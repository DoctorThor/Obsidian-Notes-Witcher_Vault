# The Ordinary Problem
This is what goes on the flier or what villagers tell the party.

It should sound:

- simple
- solvable
- probably wrong

Examples:

“A vampire drains our livestock.”
“A monster steals children at night.”
“A curse withers our crops.”
“A ghost haunts the old well.”

# The Hidden Truth
In Witcher adventures, the truth is:

- never what villagers think
- never simple
- never purely monster-caused

It’s usually one of these:

a monster acting according to its nature
a curse tied to emotion, blood, or betrayal
a spirit responding to human wrongdoing

a human trying to hide something

# The Human Sin
In Witcher stories, the monster is rarely the heart of the issue.
It’s almost always a human action that created or escalated the danger.

We now ask:
What did the village do wrong?

Examples to inspire you:

They murdered her family years ago (not knowing she survived and turned later).
They’ve been secretly executing “monstrous” looking people out of fear.
Someone in town is spreading rumors to cover their own crime.
A local priest gave a sermon that turned the village paranoid.
A villager tried to blackmail her after seeing her feeding.
A vampire hunter passed through and stirred up fear.
Her disappearance is a setup by someone who wants her dead.

# The Terrible Choice
Every Witcher story ends with a choice that:

- has no clean outcome
- forces the players to weigh humanity vs. survival
- leaves someone unhappy
- creates consequences no matter what

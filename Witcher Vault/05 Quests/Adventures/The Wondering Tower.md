`## 🧭 Quest Outline: _The Tower of John of Brugge_

**Quest Title:** _The Mage’s Shadow_  
**Setting:** Forest south of Brugge, near the Pontar River  
**Quest Type:** Exploration → Mystery → Combat/Negotiation → Moral Choice → Turn-in

---

### ⚙️ BACKGROUND (for the GM)

John of Brugge was a mage obsessed with **dimensional travel** and **mirror worlds** — a minor contemporary of Alzur. His tower vanished mysteriously over a century ago, said to “shift between worlds.”  
The book the players seek, _The Treatise on the Conjunction_, is his personal research journal — rumored to hold unstable portal formulas and dangerous theories about merging worlds.

Unknown to them, the client (a scholar or mage) **is not honest about their intentions.** They want the book to experiment with portal magic again — something forbidden by the Brotherhood of Sorcerers.

---

## 🗺️ PHASE 1 — The Forest of Echoes

**Goal:** Find the lost tower.  
**Tone:** Unease, magical distortion, natural hazards.

**Encounters:**

1. **Displaced Beasts** – Animals behave erratically. Wolves attack not out of hunger but madness; a bear might phase in and out of existence.
    
    - _Perception or Arcana Check (DC 13):_ The beasts shimmer slightly — traces of portal magic.
        
    - _Combat or avoidance opportunity._
        
2. **Echo Glade** – The party finds a clearing where voices whisper fragments of their thoughts.
    
    - Investigating reveals a **rune-inscribed obelisk**, half-buried.
        
    - _Arcana DC 14:_ Detects it as a “waystone” — one of several markers that once anchored the tower’s position in this world.
        
3. **The Phantom Path** – Following the obelisk’s runes leads to a **mirage-like shimmer** — a portal veil that flickers in and out.
    
    → _Arcana or Investigation DC 15:_ Players can stabilize the portal with a crystal, witcher sign, or magical energy source.
    

**Result:** They open a temporary gateway to John’s Tower.

---

## 🧱 PHASE 2 — The Tower of John of Brugge

**Goal:** Retrieve the book.

**Tone:** Lost time, spectral memory, experimental ruin.

Inside, the tower appears _untouched by time_. Floating candles, unfinished experiments, a half-open portal on the top floor.

**Encounters:**

1. **Specter of John** – The mage’s ghost lingers, fragmented by his own failed experiment.
    
    - He is neither hostile nor sane — his mind loops through phrases like,  
        _“The mirror must align… no, the angles are wrong, wrong again…”_
        
    - _Insight DC 13:_ Realize he’s trying to “fix” a dimensional rift.
        
2. **Puzzle Room:**
    
    - Three mirrors that must be aligned to reflect a light beam into a portal crystal.
        
    - Succeeding stabilizes the tower for a few minutes — allowing the party to move safely to the upper chamber.
        
3. **The Experiment Chamber:**
    
    - The _Treatise on the Conjunction_ lies in a stasis field beside a **mirror that shows a warped reflection of the party** — their “other selves.”
        
    - Breaking the stasis triggers combat with **a Mirror Doppelgänger** of the most magically inclined PC (CR appropriate).
        

**Outcome:**  
They recover the book, but the tower begins destabilizing — rumbling, cracking reality. They must **escape before the tower collapses into nothingness**.

---

## 🏙️ PHASE 3 — Return to Brugge

**Goal:** Deliver the book.

**Events:**

- As they near the city, a **mage hunter or sorceress’s agent** confronts them:
    
    - “John of Brugge’s works are forbidden. Hand them over.”
        
    - Offers gold or threatens to expose them for trafficking forbidden magical relics.
        
- Alternatively, the client sends thugs to “escort” them to his home instead of meeting publicly.
    

**Player Choice:**

1. **Give the book to the client** → Reward in crowns (or magical item), but a few weeks later, rumors spread of **a portal disaster near Oxenfurt**.
    
2. **Hand the book to the sorceress or hunter** → Smaller payment, but they gain a powerful ally in the Brotherhood or with a Witcher.
    
3. **Keep the book** → They now hold a dangerous artifact. Reading it could have lasting consequences or start a new arc (corruption, power, or pursuit).

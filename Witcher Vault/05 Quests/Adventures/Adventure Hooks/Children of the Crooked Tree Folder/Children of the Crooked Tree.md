# **CHILDREN OF THE CROOKED TREE**

_Deep Caed Dhu Forest, southern Duskfields – near Lyria_

A slow-burning, rural tragedy knotted in bark, blood, and grief.

---

## **HOOK**

A forest village named **Rowanrest** hides a decade-long sorrow:  
**no child has been born living for ten years.** Mothers miscarry. Infants are stillborn, silent and gray.

The villagers claim the land is cursed ever since drunken hunters slew a dryad who tried to stop them from logging a sacred grove.

A desperate elder hires the witchers, offering what little silver the village can pool.

---

## **COMPLICATION**

The curse is real, but not the cause the villagers repeat.

Long ago, a forest spirit named **Sylaen**, kin to the slain dryad, bound its grief into a living curse.  
The curse has three effects:  
• **Wombs wither** and cannot hold life  
• **Newborns are born mute** and die within hours  
• **Forest animals avoid the village**, leaving the people hungry

Sylaen lairs at the **Crooked Tree**, a once-majestic oak twisted by trauma into a monstrous spire of gnarled bark and knotted limbs. It is half-tree, half-wraith, pulsing with residual druidic magic.

As the witchers investigate, they notice:  
• Symbols carved into barns and doorframes — imitations of old dryad wards  
• Villagers hiding infant bones in household shrines  
• A woman secretly pregnant, terrified the witchers will take her child as payment

Everything in the region feels hushed, like the forest itself is holding its breath.

---

## **MORAL TWIST**

Sylaen can lift the curse…  
**but only if the roots of the Crooked Tree taste the blood of a living child.**

This is not cruelty — it is balance, an ancient law among spirits.  
A life for a life taken.  
A child for the unborn.

There _is_ one unborn child hidden in Rowanrest:  
**Elyne**, a woman seven months pregnant, kept secret by her family.  
She will beg the witchers not to let the village discover her condition.

The party must choose:  
• Give the spirit the blood it demands  
• Attempt to negotiate a new pact — difficult but possible  
• Break the curse by force, risking the forest’s wrath  
• Walk away, condemning the village to extinction

None are clean choices.

---

## **RESOLUTION OPTIONS**

### **1. Blood for Blood (The Dark Path)**

A living infant is sacrificed.  
The tree drinks deep; its branches relax; the curse ends.  
Villagers celebrate, but a pall hangs in the air: the witchers know what it cost.  
Elyne survives or does not — depending on the choice.

Sylaen whispers:  
_"Balance restores. The forest forgives… in time."_

The forest grows vibrant again within weeks.

### **2. Break the Pact (The Violent Path)**

The witchers fight Sylaen in its grove.  
The Crooked Tree becomes a battle-blighted entity: roots lash, bark bleeds sap-black tar, wraithlike tendrils rise.  
Killing the spirit ends the curse but leaves the forest scarred.  
Dryads elsewhere may learn of this. They rarely forgive.

Villagers slowly recover, but nightmares plague them — echoes of the dying spirit.

### **3. Forge a New Pact (The Clever Path)**

If the party deduces the original dryad’s remains still linger in the grove, they can:  
• Rebury them properly  
• Perform a druidic rite  
• Offer a piece of the village’s future (land, protection, cessation of logging)

This calms Sylaen enough to lift the curse **without bloodshed**, though it demands the villagers rebuild the grove and act as caretakers.

A rare Witcher-style “best ending.”

### **4. Walk Away (The Cold Path)**

Leave Rowanrest to its fate.  
Within ten years, the village becomes a ghost hamlet swallowed by Caed Dhu.  
Bards will eventually call it the **Silent Settlement**.

---

## **NPCs INVOLVED**

**Bran Varcyn**  
Gray beard, soft voice, desperate to save his people. Hides more than he reveals.  
_Motivation:_ End the curse at any cost.

**Elyne Arven**  
Pregnant woman hiding in an abandoned hunter’s lodge.  
_Motivation:_ Protect her child from both villagers and spirit.

**Tovas and Mira Keplin**  
Parents who lost three children. Mira privately considers sacrificing Elyne’s unborn child.  
_Motivation:_ Pain warps them; they want someone to blame.

**Sylaen of the Crooked Tree**  
Forest spirit, neither malicious nor kind. Ancient grief given form.  
Voice like wind through dead leaves.

**Aaron**
A young man that inhabits duskfields whose thinking about leaving.

---

## **REWARDS**

• 80–120 crowns pooled from villagers  
• A **sprig of living elderwood**, valuable to druids and mages  
• Favor with local forest spirits (if resolved peacefully)  
• A possible _dryad-made tincture_ improving signs or vitality (GM choice)  
• A lingering, haunting reputation

Optional magical reward if the party retrieves the original dryad’s heartwood shard:  
**Heartwood Charm** — grants resistance to charm and fear caused by woodland creatures.

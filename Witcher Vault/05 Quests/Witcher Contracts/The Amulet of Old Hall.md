## The Commission

*As delivered by [[Commander Varro]] at the Nilfgaard encampment outside Weddle:*

> *"The item is a medallion. Bear's head, old craftsmanship, clearly witcher-made. It was tracked to the abandoned manor on the hill — Old Hall, locals call it. I sent a witcher ahead to retrieve it quietly. He did not return. I am not a man who repeats mistakes. I need people expendable enough to go back in, capable enough to come back out, and pragmatic enough not to ask too many questions about what they're carrying. You fit that description. Five hundred orens on delivery. Not a copper less, not a copper more."*

---

## What the Commander Knows

- The amulet is a witcher medallion from the School of the Bear
- It has "significant supernatural properties" — he's been told it *creates something* but has no specifics
- A Bear School witcher named Otrygg went in three weeks ago and never came out
- Old Hall has been abandoned for roughly thirty years since the Vester family line died out
- Locals avoid it and have for years — he assumes superstition, but notes even his own soldiers won't go near it at night

## What the Commander Doesn't Know

- The amulet is a weaponized infection vector — it doesn't just create werebears, it *spreads* them
- Otrygg's ghost is still in the hall
- The original infected (the hedge mage Aldric) has been transforming for weeks and is now barely human
- Delivering the amulet gives Varro the ability to build a werebear army from prisoners, enemies, or anyone he chooses

## Background — The True History of Old Hall

**Thirty years ago:** The Vester family, minor nobility sworn to Cintra, die out. The last lord — old, no heirs — lets the manor fall into disuse. It sits on a hill above Weddle, technically crown property, practically forgotten.

**Eight months ago:** A hedge mage named **Aldric** moves into the abandoned hall. He's been chasing enchanter status for years — skilled enough to identify magic items, not skilled enough to create them. He hears rumors of a Bear School amulet stashed somewhere in the old Vester holdings, possibly left behind by a witcher who sheltered there decades prior. He finds it in a locked chest in the cellar.

**Seven months ago:** Aldric begins attempting to magically dissect the amulet. He documents everything obsessively — his notes still exist in the hall. He identifies the base medallion (Bear School, genuine) and a secondary enchantment layered on top (cruder, more recent, clearly not witcher work — a wizard's addition, purpose unclear). He attempts to absorb the secondary enchantment directly to study it.

The amulet infects him immediately. The transformation takes three days. By the end, Aldric is something that was a man. He retains enough intelligence to be dangerous and enough memory to be tragic. He cannot remove the amulet. It hangs around his neck fused to his skin.

**Three weeks ago:** The spectres already haunting the hall — echoes of the Vester household dead — have been agitated and empowered by Aldric's transformation. Two travelers who broke in looking for salvage were killed, one by the spectres, one by Aldric. Their shades now join the haunting.

**Three weeks ago:** Otrygg of Kaer Seren arrives on Varro's commission. He is good. He kills two spectres in the entry hall, makes it to the great room, identifies Aldric's scent, follows him into the cellar. Aldric ambushes him in the dark. Otrygg fights to a draw — wounds Aldric badly — but takes a killing blow. He dies on the cellar stairs. His body is still there. His silver sword lies broken beside him.

His ghost remains, bound to the hall by unfinished business and the amulet's residual power. He knows what the amulet does. He has watched the party enter. He will not help them freely.

## Old Hall — Overview

A three-storey stone manor on a low hill above Weddle. Once handsome, now collapsed in places — the east wing roof has fallen in, the stables are rubble, the gate hangs open. The main keep is structurally sound. 

**The smell:** Decay, wet stone, and underneath it something musky and animal that doesn't belong in a building.

**At night:** Lights move in the upper windows. Locals claim the old lord's family still walks the halls. They're not entirely wrong.

## Encounter 1 — The Gate Yard: The Warning
**Type:** Exploration / Roleplay / Foreshadowing
**Threat:** None (yet)

The party enters the overgrown gate yard. The main doors to the hall are ajar.

**What they find:**
- The remains of a campfire near the gate — weeks old. A bedroll, rotted through. A satchel containing a vial of **Beast Oil** (half full), a whetstone, and a torn page from a journal. This is Otrygg's camp. He never slept inside.
- Scratches on the stone gate posts — **claw marks**, deep, at varying heights. Some are clearly animal. Some are at a height that suggests something walking upright.
- A dead crow near the door, untouched by scavengers. The witcher's medallion vibrates near it. Something unnatural is very close.

**The torn journal page** (Otrygg's handwriting, readable):
> *"Bear medallion confirmed inside. Something else confirmed inside also. The claw marks on the gate are not animal — wrong spacing, wrong depth for any natural creature in this region. Smells wrong. Going in at first light. If I'm not back by—"*
> *The page tears here.*

**DM Note:** This encounter sets tone and plants questions. The party should feel the weight of the missing witcher before they see a single enemy. The PC witcher recognizes the Beast Oil as Bear School standard issue.

## Encounter 2 — The Entry Hall: The Spectres
**Type:** Combat
**Threat:** 3 Spectres (use **Shadow** stat block, CR 1/2 each, silver vulnerability added)

The entry hall is large and ruined — torn tapestries bearing the Vester family crest (a white stag), a collapsed chandelier, two staircases leading up to a mezzanine, and doors leading deeper.

Two of the spectres are old — Vester household dead, thin and faded, barely coherent. The third is newer, more defined: one of the travelers killed three weeks ago. He still wears the ghost of his traveling clothes. He screams when he attacks.

**Tactical note:** The older spectres drift through walls. The newer one cannot — he's too recently dead and too confused. He can be cornered. If the party subdues rather than destroys him with Yrden, he babbles fragmented warnings before dissipating:
> *"The big one— the big one is still DOWN there— don't go DOWN—"*

**Yrden** traps spectres in place for one round, making them vulnerable to silver. **Igni** does half damage. Non-silver weapons do nothing.

**What the ghost witcher does here:** Otrygg watches from the mezzanine shadows. The party may notice a faint shape — vaguely human, very still — that disappears when they look directly at it. He does not speak. He does not help.

**Loot:** Nothing of value. The Vester tapestries are too rotted to sell. A careful search of the hall finds a **silver candlestick** (worth 15 orens, also functions as an improvised silver weapon, 1d4 damage).

## Encounter 3 — The Great Room: The Puzzle
**Type:** Puzzle / Investigation
**Threat:** None, but failure has consequences

The great room was once the heart of the manor — long dining table (collapsed), fireplace (cold), and bookshelves lining the walls. Aldric used this as his study before he transformed. His work is everywhere.

**What's here:**
- A **large worktable** covered in Aldric's notes, diagrams, and partially disassembled magic trinkets
- A **locked iron box** bolted to the table (this is where Aldric kept the amulet before it took him)
- A **magical ward** on the door leading to the study — Aldric set it to protect his research. It triggers if the wrong person tries to pass.

**Aldric's Notes — what the party can learn:**
A DC 14 Intelligence (Arcana) check to read and understand them quickly, DC 10 with more time:
- The amulet has two distinct magical signatures — the base medallion and a secondary enchantment
- The secondary enchantment is *viral in nature* — it rewrites the subject's biology
- His last entry, three days before full transformation: *"The secondary layer is not a ward or an enhancement. It is a SEED. I have made a catastrophic error. Do not touch the amulet directly. Do NOT—"* The handwriting degrades here into something barely legible, then stops.

**The Ward Puzzle:**
The door to the study has a ward Aldric keyed to *intellectual intent* — crude but functional. Three symbols carved above the door: an open book, a closed fist, and an eye.

- Touching the closed fist triggers the ward: **DC 15 Dex save or take 3d8 lightning damage**
- Touching the open book does nothing
- Touching the eye does nothing
- **Solution:** Touch the open book, then the eye, in that order. "To see, one must first be willing to learn." A DC 13 Intelligence (History or Arcana) check lets a character deduce this from Aldric's notes, which reference his belief that *knowledge precedes understanding*. The PC witcher has advantage on this check — Bear School training emphasizes research before action.

**DM Note:** The party doesn't have to solve this elegantly. They can take the lightning damage and push through. But solving it gives them a moment of feeling like they *understand* Aldric — which makes the cellar harder.

## Encounter 4 — The Study: The Trap
**Type:** Trap / Skill Challenge
**Threat:** Environmental

Aldric's inner sanctum. More notes, a cot, personal effects. A window overlooking the cellar entrance below. And a **tripwire** Aldric set across the threshold after his transformation began — a moment of lucidity in which he tried to slow down anyone who might find him.

**The Tripwire:**
A thin silver wire (DC 16 Perception to spot) connected to a counterweight above the door. If triggered, it drops a **iron portcullis** that seals the study door and simultaneously opens a trapdoor in the floor — dropping whoever triggered it 10 feet into a **refuse pit** (2d6 bludgeoning, DC 12 Con save or stunned for one round from the stench).

**What's in the study worth finding:**
- **Aldric's Final Notes** — a separate, sealed journal. DC 12 Intelligence to understand quickly. Contains: the full picture of what the amulet does, the fact that it cannot be removed from the infected host without killing them, and a desperate half-finished attempt to write a counter-enchantment that he never completed. Crucially: *"The amulet's infection requires direct skin contact for at least one minute. Brief handling is safe. I wore it for six. God help me."*
- **A vial of Specter Oil** (full) — Aldric harvested it from a witcher's formula he found in the Vester library
- **A letter, unsealed, never sent** — addressed to "My sister in Vizima." It reads simply: *"I have done something I cannot undo. Do not come looking for me. Tell mother I thought of her at the end."* No signature.

**DM Note:** The letter is not a plot item. It is just a human moment. Let the party sit with it.

## Encounter 5 — The Cellar Stairs: Otrygg's Remains
**Type:** Roleplay / Revelation
**Threat:** None

The cellar stairs descend steeply. Halfway down, in the torchlight, the party finds **Otrygg**.

He died sitting against the wall, back to the stone, facing upward. His silver sword is broken in two beside him — the tip snapped off, still embedded somewhere in Aldric below. He is wearing Bear School armor, identifiable by the carved bear motif on the chest plate. His medallion — a bear's head, standard issue — still hangs around his neck. His wounds are massive: three parallel gashes across his torso, the kind made by something with very large claws.

**He looks like he sat down to rest and simply didn't get up.**

**The PC Witcher's Moment:**
When the PC witcher approaches and acknowledges Otrygg — whether they speak to him, remove their own medallion in respect, close his eyes, or simply crouch beside him and take a moment — **Otrygg's ghost manifests**.

He appears as he looked in life: middle-aged, broad-shouldered, a witcher's blank calm on his face. But there is exhaustion behind his eyes that has nothing to do with death.

**Otrygg's Testimony** (delivered quietly, without drama):
> *"I wondered which of you would be the witcher. You carry yourself like Kaer Seren trained you. Good."*
>
> *"The amulet is on the thing below. The hedge mage. He's been down there since I wounded him — he heals fast but I took something out of him. He won't be easy."*
>
> *"The amulet cannot be taken from him while he lives. It has fused to his skin. You either kill him and take it, or you leave it here."*
>
> *"The man who sent you — the Nilfgaardian — he doesn't know what it does. He thinks it's a weapon. He's right. But he's imagining a sword. What he's actually holding is a plague."*
>
> *"I was going to bring it back. That was the job. I've had thirty years to think about whether that was the right call. It wasn't."*
>
> *"What you do with it is your business. I'm done making that choice for other people."*

**If asked how to destroy it:**
> *"I don't know. I spent three weeks trying to figure that out. The enchantment is layered into the base medallion — you can't separate them. Melting it down might work. Might not. Might just spread the infection to whoever's in the room when the metal goes liquid."*

**If the PC witcher asks about the Bear School:**
> *"Tell them Otrygg of Kaer Seren fulfilled his contract. The amulet is retrieved. Don't tell them how."*
> *He pauses.*
> *"Tell them I said the schools should talk to each other more."*

**After delivering his testimony, Otrygg does not disappear immediately.** He stays, watching, until the party descends into the cellar. When they return — with or without the amulet, whether Aldric is dead or alive — he is gone. The only sign he was there is that his broken sword has been moved: the two pieces placed carefully together, as if someone tried to make it whole again.

## Encounter 6 — The Cellar: Aldric
**Type:** Boss Combat / Moral Climax
**Threat:** Aldric, the Uber-Werebear

The cellar is large — old wine storage, mostly empty racks, a dirt floor. It smells overwhelmingly of animal. Scratch marks cover every surface. In the far corner, curled around a support pillar like an enormous sleeping dog, is **Aldric**.

He is massive. The transformation is not clean or romantic — this is not a noble beast. He is too large, wrong proportions, the remnants of his robes stretched and torn across a body that was redesigned by something that didn't care about aesthetics. The amulet glows faintly at his throat, fused into the skin.

**He is awake.** He has been awake since the party entered the hall. He has been listening.

**The Opening Beat:**
Aldric does not attack immediately. He raises his head and looks at the party. His eyes are wrong — too intelligent for an animal, too animal for a man. When he speaks, it is slow and effortful, like someone speaking through a mouth that no longer fits the words:

> *"...You read... my notes."*

It is not a question.

> *"Then you know... what this is."*
> *"...Take it. Please."*

**The Tragedy:**
Aldric wants to die. He has wanted to die for months. He cannot remove the amulet himself — he has tried, the scarring on his hands shows it. He cannot leave the cellar — some compulsion tied to the amulet keeps him here. He has been waiting for someone to come and end it.

He will not fight back unless attacked with the intent to flee rather than kill — if the party tries to grab the amulet and run, his instincts override his will and he attacks. He is not in control of himself at that point.

**If the party attempts to communicate:**
A DC 14 Charisma (Persuasion) check — with advantage if the PC witcher does the talking, or if the party referenced his notes — allows them to keep him calm through the encounter. On a success, Aldric holds himself still and allows himself to be killed. On a failure, he loses coherence mid-conversation and the fight begins.

**The Fight (if it comes to it):**

**TRAITS**

**Amulet Bond.** The amulet cannot be removed from Aldric while he lives. Any attempt to forcibly remove it causes Aldric to immediately use his Rampage reaction on the creature that tried, regardless of other triggers.

**Regeneration.** Aldric regains 5 hit points at the start of his turn. If he takes silver weapon damage, this trait doesn't function at the start of his next turn. He dies only if he starts his turn with 0 hit points and doesn't regenerate.

**Keen Smell.** Aldric has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell. He always knows where every creature in the cellar is, even in total darkness.

**Wounded and Slow.** Aldric was badly injured by Otrygg three weeks ago and has not fully healed despite his regeneration — Otrygg's silver blade left wounds that close but never fully seal. When Aldric takes silver weapon damage, his speed is reduced by 10 ft. until the end of his next turn.

**Lucid Moment (1/encounter).** When first reduced below 30 HP, Aldric regains enough coherence to speak one clear sentence before losing it again.

---

**ACTIONS**

**Multiattack.** Aldric makes two attacks: one Bite and one Claws.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 11 (2d6 + 4) piercing damage. If the target is Medium or smaller, it must succeed on a DC 14 Strength saving throw or be grappled (escape DC 14).

**Claws.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit:* 13 (2d8 + 4) slashing damage.

**Infectious Swipe (Recharge 5–6).** Aldric makes one Claws attack. On a hit, in addition to normal damage, the target must succeed on a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw** or become **Infected** (see below). This represents the amulet's corruption radiating outward through Aldric's body — not the amulet itself.

---

**BONUS ACTIONS**

**Rampage.** When Aldric reduces a creature to 0 hit points with a melee attack, he can move up to 10 feet and make one Bite attack against another creature within reach.

---

**REACTIONS**

**Cornered Beast.** When Aldric is reduced to 25 HP or fewer for the first time, he lets out a low, broken sound — not quite a roar — and makes one immediate Claws attack against the creature that dealt the triggering damage. This attack is made with disadvantage as his body fights against his own instinct to hurt someone.

*This is the moment. His hand shakes during the attack. He is fighting himself.*

---

**TACTICAL NOTES FOR THE DM**

Aldric fights defensively and reactively rather than aggressively — he is not hunting the party, he is enduring them. He prioritizes grappling the most physically imposing target to neutralize them, then uses Claws on whoever is dealing the most damage. He never pursues a fleeing creature out of the cellar.

He will not use Infectious Swipe on Spiros (the witcher) if he can tell Spiros is Bear School — he recognizes the medallion on some level his animal mind still processes. He will use it freely on everyone else.

**Silver weapons** are the key. Without silver, the party will struggle to push past his regeneration — they can wear him down but it will be a grind. With silver, he is manageable. The candlestick from Encounter 2 becomes tactically relevant here.

**The Persuasion option** (DC 14, advantage if Spiros leads or party referenced his notes) remains available throughout the fight as a bonus action for any character who speaks to him. A successful check causes Aldric to use his Cornered Beast reaction against himself on his next turn — his claws dragging across his own chest as his will overrides his instinct, dealing 9 (2d8) damage to himself. He can only be talked through this twice before his intelligence degrades completely.

---

**THE INFECTED CONDITION**

A creature that fails the save against Infectious Swipe begins a slow transformation. Symptoms appear after a long rest. Full transformation takes one week without intervention.

*Day 1–2:* Advantage on Strength checks and saving throws. Disadvantage on all Intelligence checks. Sleep is disturbed — vivid, violent dreams. No mechanical penalty yet, but the player should feel it.

*Day 3–4:* Uncontrolled aggression surfaces. When the infected character takes damage, they must succeed on a **DC 12 Wisdom saving throw** or use their reaction to make one melee attack against the nearest creature, friend or foe.

*Day 5–6:* Physical changes become visible — increased body hair, thickened fingers, altered posture. Size category increases by one. The character has disadvantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks as their appearance unnerves people.

*Day 7:* On the night of the seventh day, the character makes a final **DC 15 Wisdom saving throw**. On a success, the transformation halts — they are permanently changed but retain their mind and remain a PC, albeit one with a werebear's physiology and a very complicated future. On a failure, full transformation completes and the character becomes an NPC werebear.

**Curing the Infection:** Aldric's notes contain a partial counter-formula requiring three ingredients found in the Cintra region: **blackthorn root** (common, any herbalist), **harpy feathers** (requires a small hunt or purchase from a trader), and **echo moss** (grows only near sites of old magical activity — there is a source near the ruins of a pre-Conjunction elven outpost two days from Weddle). A character with proficiency in Medicine or the herbalism kit can complete the formula from the notes with a DC 15 Intelligence check. Alternatively, *Remove Curse* cast at 3rd level works, but causes one level of exhaustion per day since infection.

**Curing the Infection:** Aldric's notes contain a partial formula for a counter-poultice. A skilled herbalist or mage could complete it — DC 18 Medicine or Arcana check with Aldric's notes in hand, requiring three rare ingredients found in the Cintra region. Alternatively, a powerful cleric's Remove Curse (cast at 5th level or higher) works, but causes the infected character severe pain and one level of exhaustion per day since infection.

**ALDRIC'S LUCID MOMENT**

When first reduced below 30 HP, Aldric goes still for one breath. He looks at the party — really looks, the human intelligence surfacing one last time. He says, clearly:

> *"...Don't give it to the soldier."*

Then it's gone.

## Taking the Amulet

When Aldric dies, the amulet releases from his skin with an audible sound — like a sigh. It drops to the dirt floor. The glow fades.

Handling it briefly is safe (per Aldric's notes — one minute of direct skin contact required for infection). It is cold to the touch, heavier than it looks. The bear's head on the medallion is exquisite work. The secondary enchantment is visible to anyone with magical sensitivity — a faint oily shimmer over the surface, clearly different from the clean witcher craftsmanship underneath.

**For the PC witcher:** DC 12 Arcana check to confirm what the notes said — the secondary enchantment is not Bear School work. Someone took a genuine witcher medallion and corrupted it. The base medallion is, or was, a real piece of their school's history.

## The Moral Decision

The party leaves Old Hall with the amulet. [[Commander Varro]] is waiting.

### Option 1 — Deliver It
Full payment: 500 orens. Varro examines it with satisfaction, asks no questions, dismisses them politely. He will spend the next several months figuring out how to use it. In a future session, consequences arrive — a report of werebear attacks on Cintran border villages, prisoners of the garrison gone missing, something that connects back to this moment.

### Option 2 — Refuse / Lie
The party claims they couldn't retrieve it, or that it was destroyed. Varro is a polite man but not a naive one. DC 15 Deception to convince him. On a failure, he doesn't attack immediately — he is a nobleman and there are witnesses — but he notes their names and faces. The party has made an enemy of the Nilfgaardian garrison. In this era and this region, that has weight.

### Option 3 — Destroy It
Aldric's notes suggest smelting might work, might not. Attempting it requires a forge hot enough (a blacksmith's furnace), produces a toxic vapor (DC 14 Con save or poisoned for 24 hours), and has a 50% chance of simply not working — the amulet survives, slightly warped. A success destroys it permanently. Varro receives nothing and knows it — see Option 2 consequences.

### Option 4 — Keep It
The amulet goes in a pocket. For now. The clock on "what do we do with this" starts ticking. Every session it remains with the party is a session where someone could accidentally handle it too long, where Varro's people could come looking, where the PC witcher's conscience gets louder.

## Non-Human Party Considerations

This quest has specific texture for the elf and dwarves that should be woven in throughout:

**At the commission:** Varro is polite to everyone. This is almost more unsettling than hostility would be. Nilfgaard's politeness is the politeness of an empire that has already decided the outcome — courtesy costs them nothing. The elf and dwarves should feel the weight of being hired help to a power that views their people as a subject population at best.

**In Old Hall:** The Vester family tapestries, if examined, show hunting scenes. One shows humans hunting elves — framed as sport, clearly old. This was normal art for this class, in this era. It's just hanging on a wall.

**Aldric's notes:** He refers to his hedge mage colleagues by name, mentions a dwarven alchemist in Cintra who sold him reagents, treats non-humans as normal professional contacts. He was not a bad man. This matters.

**The decision:** For the elf especially — giving this weapon to Nilfgaard is giving a human empire a tool to make more monsters. The question of whether non-humans suffer more or less under Nilfgaard than under the Northern Kingdoms is complicated, and this is a small moment where that question becomes real.

## DM Notes

*The thematic spine of this quest: everyone involved — Otrygg, Aldric, Varro, even the party — is doing what seems rational given what they know. The tragedy is in the gaps between what each person understands. Otrygg took a job without asking what it was for. Aldric pursued knowledge without respecting what he didn't know. Varro wants a weapon without understanding what weapons do to the people who use them. The party is the first group with enough information to actually make a real choice. Whether they make the right one is up to them.*

*Play Varro as genuinely pleasant. Never threatening. The threat is entirely contextual — he doesn't need to make it.*

*Play Otrygg as tired, not tragic. He has had weeks to process his death. He is past grief. He is just telling them what he knows.*

*Play Aldric as small inside the monster. The intelligence in his eyes matters more than the claws.*

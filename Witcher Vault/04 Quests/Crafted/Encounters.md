### 1. **The Hanged Company**

Along a war-scarred road, the players find half a dozen **Nilfgaardian deserters** hanged from trees, black uniforms still intact. Crows pick at their eyes.

- A soldier’s journal dangles from one corpse’s belt — inside, a note warns of “a thing that stalks soldiers at night, wearing their faces.”
    
- That night, something with a soldier’s voice calls for help from outside their campfire.
    

🩸 _Possible foes:_ Doppler, Hym, or a Penitent  
⚖️ _Choice:_ Do they bury the dead or leave them to rot — and risk angering the spirit that still guards them?

---

### 2. **The Witch’s Toll**

A rickety bridge crosses a flooded ravine. A **swamp witch** demands “a toll of life” to cross — a lock of hair, a drop of blood, or a memory.

- Paying peacefully grants safe passage (and possibly a minor blessing).
    
- Refusing may awaken **water hags** in the river below.
    

🎭 _Twist:_ The witch’s “toll” is actually a protective charm keeping a worse spirit sealed beneath the bridge. Killing her breaks the ward.

---

### 3. **Smoke on the Horizon**

A small hamlet burns in the distance. Survivors claim **a wyvern** attacked their livestock, and panicked villagers accidentally set their own homes ablaze trying to fight it.

- The wyvern is nesting nearby, wounded, guarding its young.
    
- Villagers will pay in coin or goods if the players bring proof it’s dead.
    

⚖️ _Choice:_ Slay the beast for the reward, or drive it off and risk the villagers’ anger.  
💰 _Reward:_ 50–100 crowns or valuable supplies.

---

### 4. **A Bard’s Debt**

On the road, the group meets a **bard named Ilaris**, charming but desperate. He begs for protection from bandits he owes money to.

- If helped, he repays them later with gossip, songs, and rumors of rare contracts.
    
- If refused, the next village over has news that a bard was found mutilated — and his ghost haunts the roadside at night.
    

🎭 _Roleplay Hook:_ Ilaris may return in any future quest as comic relief or a tragic echo.

---

### 5. **The Corpse Wagon**

A **plague wagon** creaks along the road, drawn by two weary horses. A masked man offers coin if the players help him bury the dead.

- Some bodies move — **ghouls** feasting among the corpses.
    
- The “plague” may be a necrotic curse spread by magic rather than disease.
    

💀 _Choice:_ Help the man cleanse the area or burn everything, earning fear (and fame) from nearby villages.

---

### 6. **The Stone Shrine**

In a misty clearing stands an old **Melitele shrine**, cracked and overgrown. Local peasants leave offerings of flowers and blood.

- Staying overnight brings strange dreams — a woman weeping beside a black lake.
    
- Investigating reveals a **noonwraith’s** haunting caused by a murdered priestess.
    

🕯️ _Choice:_ Free the spirit with a ritual (costly ingredients) or destroy it and risk divine disfavor.

---

### 7. **The Hungry Road**

Travelers whisper of a road that “eats” those who stray too far from its center.

- At dusk, the trees seem to move — **leshy roots** drag corpses into the earth.
    
- Old witcher markings warn “Do not light fires here.”
    

🌲 _Choice:_ Camp in the open and risk monsters, or press through the night and risk exhaustion.

---

### 8. **The Blighted Mill**

A windmill turns slowly in the fog. Inside are half-eaten grain sacks, blood, and claw marks.

- A **botchling** hides inside the grain chute, born of a stillborn miller’s child never given proper burial.
    
- Proper rites could turn it into a **lubberkin** who guards the party from harm.
    

💀 _Choice:_ Banish the creature or sanctify it — the latter costs empathy, but grants a supernatural protector for the next leg of travel.

---

### 9. **The Lost Knight**

An armored corpse kneels beside a road shrine, sword driven into the earth.

- The knight’s ghost appears after dark, begging the players to deliver his blade to his liege in Vizima.
    
- The sword, however, is **cursed** — it draws the attention of specters and drains the wielder’s vitality.
    

⚖️ _Choice:_ Deliver the sword and end the curse, or keep it for its deadly power.

---

### 10. **The Dead Feast**

The group comes upon an abandoned roadside inn where the smell of food still lingers.

- The tables are set, but the patrons are all long-dead.
    
- As night falls, their spirits rise and invite the party to “eat with us.” Refusing angers them; joining the feast traps them in a time loop.
    

👻 _Possible Solution:_ Pour out wine in offering and burn the inn down — the only way to free the dead from their eternal supper.

## Character Summary

Zinbalar Mistwhisper is a High Half-Elf bard from Dol Blathanna — fair-skinned, amber-eyed, chestnut-brown haired, 6 feet tall and 120 lbs. College of Lore means he collects knowledge the way other people collect wounds — deliberately, carefully, and with an eye toward using it later. His parents were a musician and a jewelry craftsman, which explains both the performance skills and the eye for detail. He is neutral in alignment and faithful to Melitile, which in the Witcher setting says something about how he thinks about fate and destiny. He travels with a non-human party in human territory, and as a half-elf he occupies the uncomfortable middle ground — too human for some elves, too elven for most humans.

---

## Appearance

**Height / Build:** 6'0", 120 lbs. Lean. Moves quietly.
**Distinguishing Features:** Fair skin, amber eyes, chestnut-brown hair. Half-elven features — pointed ears, slightly too fine-boned to pass as fully human on close inspection. Carries a hand drum. Dresses in costume clothing when performing, common clothes otherwise.
**How they present in hostile territory:** He can pass as human at a distance, which he probably does when it's useful and resents when he has to. The half-elf position — not quite belonging anywhere — is a character note that should surface in how he navigates human settlements.

## Personality & Roleplay Notes

**Core traits:** Neutral. Melitile faith. Pragmatic, observant, information-oriented.
**Under pressure:** Uses Bardic Inspiration and Cutting Words to control outcomes before they happen. Prefers to shape a situation rather than react to it.
**Toward non-humans:** Complex — he's half-elven, from Dol Blathanna, which means he has ties to the elven world and the complicated politics that come with it. More on this as the campaign develops.
**Toward humans:** Cautious familiarity. He can move in human spaces. He knows their music, their stories, their tells. That doesn't mean he trusts them.
**Toward authority:** Neutral — he observes authority rather than challenging or serving it. Information about powerful people is useful. Antagonising them is not.
**Toward the witcher:** A College of Lore bard and an Eldritch Knight witcher have more in common than either might admit. Both are knowledge-gatherers. Whether that creates rapport or competition is to be developed.
**Secrets or things they haven't told the party:** Parents in Dol Blathanna — alive, not important yet. What brought him this far from home. What he's actually collecting information for.

## Background & History

### Before the Campaign
Born in Dol Blathanna. Parents are alive — a musician and a jewelry craftsman — neither significant figures yet. The Sage background combined with College of Lore suggests formal study somewhere, or at least a systematic approach to self-education. A half-elf from Dol Blathanna in the 1240–1260 timeframe is someone who grew up in the aftermath of the elven retreat into that valley — aware of what the elves lost, aware of what humans did, living in the space between.

### Why He's Here
Far from Dol Blathanna, traveling with a mixed non-human party. What drove him out and what he's looking for is to be developed through play.

## Non-Human Situation

**Race and how it reads in human territory:** Can pass at distance but not on close inspection. The pointed ears and elven bone structure mark him once someone looks closely. In Cintra and the borderlands, half-elves are not uncommon enough to be exotic but not common enough to be comfortable.
**How they handle the gate tax / refused service:** Persuasion +7 with Expertise. He talks his way through. He is very good at talking his way through.
**History with human institutions:** Unknown — to be developed.
**Relationship with their own people:** Half-elf from Dol Blathanna. His relationship with the elven community there — whether he was accepted, tolerated, or always on the outside — is a character thread.

## Party Dynamic

**Relationship with Spiros (Witcher):** Both are knowledge-gatherers. Both have Sage backgrounds. Whether this creates natural alliance or quiet competition is to be developed.
**Relationship with Indra Taran:** The face of the party next to the storm. Complementary — Zinbalar smooths the path Indra would otherwise walk through.
**Relationship with Frixion:** Two craftsmen's children, one from Mahakam and one from Dol Blathanna. The shared working-class origin under the surface-level differences is a potential bond.
**Role in the party dynamic:** The voice. The one who talks to people, reads rooms, and knows things. When the party needs information or a way out of a social situation, Zinbalar is the answer.
**Tension points:** The half-elf's position between worlds. What he owes to elven heritage versus what he's willing to do to survive in human territory. What he's actually collecting information for.

## Character Summary

Spiros Dawnspear is a Bear School witcher — a Variant Human Fighter (Eldritch Knight) whose magical abilities represent the Signs and alchemical knowledge of his training. Tall, pale-skinned, green-eyed, red-haired. Intelligent in combat, tactically minded, arrogant about it. He is the only human in a non-human party, which is itself a social and political statement in the Northern Kingdoms. His bond is his sword and a friend in Skellige. His flaw is arrogance in combat — the witcher who always thinks he has already solved the problem.

---

## Appearance

**Height / Build:** 6'2", 210 lbs. Athletic, long-limbed. Moves like someone trained to move.
**Distinguishing Features:** Pale skin. Green eyes. Red hair. The Bear School medallion — the detail that identified the amulet in the current quest and marks him immediately as a witcher to anyone who knows what they're looking at.
**How they present in hostile territory:** He's human, which means he passes. But a witcher traveling with dwarves and a half-elf draws its own kind of attention — questions, suspicion, the occasional insult aimed at the company he keeps.

---

## Personality & Roleplay Notes

**Core traits:** Chaotic Good. No faith listed. Intelligent, tactical, assesses situations quickly.
**Under pressure:** Goes for the optimal solution. May not consult the party first.
**Toward non-humans:** Travels with them willingly — that's already a statement. Pragmatic rather than idealistic about it.
**Toward humans:** Complicated. He is one, but witchers occupy their own social category — feared, useful, tolerated, rarely welcomed.
**Toward authority:** Chaotic Good witcher. Draw your own conclusions.
**Toward the party:** The senior member by level and by professional experience. Whether he leads or simply acts like he leads is a tension point.
**Secrets or things they haven't told the party:** The friend in Skellige. What that bond means and what it might cost him.

## Background & History

### Before the Campaign
A Bear School witcher. Trained at Kaer Seren — the same keep where Otrygg of Kaer Seren was trained, which is the reason he recognised the medallion in the current quest. 80 years old, which for a witcher is middle-experienced. The Sage background means he pursued knowledge beyond the standard witcher curriculum — magical theory, history, something that made him more than just a sword.

### Why He's Here
Witchers take contracts. This is a contract. Whether there's more to it than that — whether Kaer Seren sent him specifically, whether Otrygg's death is personal — is to be developed through play.

### Family & Connections
- **Friend in Skellige** — named as his Bond. Identity, nature of friendship, and current status to be developed through play.
- **Kaer Seren** — Bear School keep. Institutional home, not family, but carries weight.
- **Otrygg of Kaer Seren** — fellow Bear School witcher, now dead in the cellar of Old Hall. His ghost is waiting.

---

## Non-Human Situation

**Race and how it reads in human territory:** He's human. He passes. But the witcher's medallion marks him as something humans distrust for different reasons — mutation, monster-slaying, the mutations that make his eyes catch light wrong in the dark.
**How they handle the gate tax / refused service:** He doesn't pay it personally, but he's traveling with people who do. How he responds to that — intervenes, lets it go, uses his witcher status to argue — defines his relationship with the party.
**History with human institutions:** Witchers exist in a complicated relationship with all institutions. The Brotherhood tolerates them. Nobles use them. Common people fear them. Nilfgaard has its own opinions.
**Relationship with their own people:** Witchers don't have people in the conventional sense. Bear School is the closest thing.

---

## Party Dynamic

**Relationship with Indra Taran:** The witcher and the storm dwarf. He has the tactical mind; she has the force of nature. Whether they complement or clash depends on who's making decisions.
**Relationship with Zinbalar:** A bard is useful. A bard with College of Lore is very useful. Spiros probably appreciates the information-gathering more than the performance.
**Relationship with Frixion:** An artificer who builds magical armor is functionally a hedge mage with better tools. Spiros has opinions about magic. Whether he respects Frixion's approach or condescends to it is a live question.
**Role in the party dynamic:** The professional. The one who has done this before. The one who recognized the medallion and knew what it meant before anyone asked.
**Tension points:** Arrogance in combat. The friend in Skellige. What it means to be human in a non-human party without making it about himself.

## Character Summary

 [[Curse of the Lost Knight|Cursed]]
Frixion is a Mountain Dwarf Artificer specialising in the Armorer subclass — Guardian model. He builds his magic into his armor and fights from inside it, which suits a dwarf who is simultaneously a craftsman, a snob, and someone who is horribly jealous of anyone who outshines his handiwork. He has a significant outstanding debt — 1,225 crowns owed to someone named Anton — which is either a motivation, a complication, or both. His workshop is the most important place in the world to him. He is Chaotic Neutral, committed to the people he cares about rather than to ideals, and everywhere he goes he is surrounded by people who cannot appreciate fine art.

## Personality & Roleplay Notes

**Core traits:** Chaotic Neutral. A snob about craftsmanship. Does not part with money easily. Haggles tirelessly. Committed to the people he cares about, not to abstract ideals.
**Under pressure:** Deploys the Guardian Armor's Defensive Field, gets into melee with the Thunder Gauntlets, and probably complains about the damage to his work afterward.
**Toward non-humans:** Travels with them. Mountain Dwarves in human territory face similar pressures to other non-humans — probably more pragmatic about it than idealistic.
**Toward humans:** Transactional. Will work with anyone who can pay and appreciate quality work. Will not forgive anyone who dismisses his craftsmanship.
**Toward authority:** Chaotic Neutral with a guild background. He respects the authority of expertise and craft. Everything else is negotiable.
**Toward the witcher:** A witcher is a professional who maintains specialised equipment under difficult conditions. Frixion probably has opinions about the quality of Spiros's gear.
**Secrets or things they haven't told the party:** 1,225 crowns owed to Anton. Who is Anton, what the debt is for, and whether Anton is looking for him are live questions.

**Race and how it reads in human territory:** Mountain Dwarf — stocky, broad, immediately recognisable. Not as visually striking as Indra's stone arms or Zinbalar's elven features, but dwarves in the Cintran borderlands face the standard non-human tax and attitude.
**How they handle the gate tax / refused service:** He haggles. He is constitutionally incapable of not haggling. This probably goes worse before it goes better.

## Party Dynamic

**Relationship with Spiros (Witcher):** Has opinions about Spiros's equipment. Whether Spiros welcomes or resents that is a live question.
**Relationship with Indra Taran:** Two dwarves. Different kinds of dwarves — one a storm barbarian, one a craftsman-mage. The dynamic has potential for both solidarity and friction.

**Tension points:** The debt to Anton. The missing workshop. The jealousy toward anyone who outshines his work — which in a party with a thunder dwarf, a half-elf bard, and a witcher, is everyone at least some of the time.

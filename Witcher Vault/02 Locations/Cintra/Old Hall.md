## Overview

Old Hall is an abandoned manor estate on the outskirts of Weddle, a small village in the Cintran borderlands. It was purchased two decades ago by Aldric Wehn, a hedge mage and former associate of the Brotherhood of Sorcerers, who retired here to pursue independent magical research. His work on a recovered Bear School witcher medallion — an artifact capable of transmitting lycanthropic infection — resulted in his own accidental transformation into a werebear. He has been trapped here, unable to leave, unwilling to harm others, slowly losing himself.

The manor is four stories above ground (ground floor, second floor, third floor, attic) with an excavated undercroft below. Aldric excavated the undercroft himself, over months of partially-lucid effort, to give his transformed self enough space to survive without destroying the house or reaching the village.

A Bear School witcher named Otrygg of Kaer Seren attempted this job before the current party and died in the undercroft. His ghost remains, anchored to his own remains on the cellar stairs, and will not speak until his body is acknowledged with proper rite.

## Non-Human Situation

Old Hall itself is neutral ground — no human authority operates inside the manor. The village of Weddle has the usual tensions, but the manor's isolation means the party faces no direct gate tax or racial gatekeeping once inside. However: Aldric is not hostile to non-humans and will address the party's dwarves and elf without prejudice. He has been alone long enough to have no patience for bigotry.

## Monster Presence

- **Aldric Wehn** — CR 4 werebear. Main cellar. Sentient, communicative, wants to die. Does not deal killing blows.

- **Three Shades** — Use Shadow stat block (MM p. 269). Entry Hall (Ground Floor Room 5). Anchored. Silver vulnerability added.

- **Otrygg's Ghost** — Non-hostile. Cellar stairs. Witcher of Kaer Seren. Triggers on acknowledgment of remains.

## Moral Complication

Commander Varro commissioned this job for an unstated purpose. Aldric's notes, Otrygg's testimony, and the letters in the study collectively reveal: the Empire wants the amulet as an infection vector weapon. Aldric can be saved if the ritual in the attic is completed (requires a silvered magical component not yet present in the manor). The amulet can be destroyed in the reflection pool. Delivering the amulet to Varro arms Nilfgaard with a tool for creating werebear shock troops or contaminating populations.

The party must decide: deliver for 800 NC, destroy and face Varro's response, or find a third path.

## Ground Floor

*The ground floor smells of rot and old smoke. Every surface is filmed with dust thick enough to write in. Floorboards creak underfoot with the particular protest of wood that has given up caring. The windows are shuttered from inside — someone closed them carefully before they left, or before something stopped them from leaving.*


### 1A — The Gate Yard

> [!warning] Encounter Zone
> ENCOUNTER ZONE 1 — Foreshadowing and Otrygg's camp

The iron gate to Old Hall stands open, one hinge bent outward as though something large pushed through it from inside. Otrygg's camp sits just inside the yard — a bedroll stiff with dried blood, a rusted lantern, a witcher's pack with the buckles torn off. His silver sword is still in its scabbard, propped against the garden wall as though he left it there on purpose. The grounds are overgrown. A kitchen garden has gone entirely to seed. Something large has walked through the flower beds — the impressions are old, but deep.

**GM Notes:** Otrygg's silver sword is here and usable. It's a functional silver sword, slightly inferior to a master-crafted weapon — treat as a longsword that deals silvered damage. Spiros will recognize the Bear School gear immediately. Investigation DC 12 reveals the gate was forced outward, not inward. The footprints in the garden are enormous — DC 14 Survival to identify as werebear. The bedroll blood is old but extensive. Otrygg did not leave voluntarily.

**Sensory Details:**
- The gate groans in the wind
- Smell of old blood under the garden rot
- Otrygg's pack has a carved bear medallion on the inside flap — Spiros recognizes the work

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Otrygg's silver sword (functional, silvered longsword)
- Witcher's pack — trail rations, alchemical components (2x Swallow equivalent, 1x Cat equivalent), 45 NC in coin


### 1B — The Receiving Hall

The receiving hall beyond the front door is wide and cold. A coat rack stands near the door, still hung with a heavy wool cloak — good quality, a gentleman's garment, but mildewed through. The floor is black-and-white tile, cracked along one diagonal as though the house settled hard. A portrait hangs on the west wall: a middle-aged man with a scholar's soft hands and a nervous smile, standing before a fireplace. The brass nameplate beneath it reads ALDRIC WEHN. Someone has scratched through the surname with something sharp.

**GM Notes:** The portrait is Aldric before the transformation. Players who later see him in the cellar can connect the face if they remember it. The scratched-out surname is Aldric's own work — he erased it during a moment of self-recrimination. Investigation DC 10 reveals the scratching was done from a low angle, as though by someone crouching or kneeling. The cloak has a tailor's mark from a Cintran merchant — establishes Aldric as a local.

**Sensory Details:**
- Cold air despite no open windows
- The tile crack runs toward the cellar door
- Faint smell of old pipeweed


### 2A — The Sitting Room

A sitting room in advanced decay. Two chairs face a fireplace that hasn't been lit in years, the grate full of ash and a single burned book — only the leather cover survives. A side table holds the remains of a wine service: two glasses, one tipped over and shattered, one still upright with a residue of dark sediment at the bottom. The curtains have rotted from their rings and pooled on the floor like a shed skin.

**GM Notes:** The burned book in the grate is deliberate — Aldric burned his personal correspondence before things got bad. The wine residue in the standing glass contains trace alchemical compounds. DC 15 Arcana or DC 13 Medicine identifies it as a failed attempt at a dilution agent — someone was trying to neutralize something they'd ingested. This is an early clue that Aldric was trying to cure himself.

**Sensory Details:**
- Ash smell, cold and stale
- One chair has claw marks on the armrests — something gripped it very hard
- A moth-eaten hunting dog effigy on the mantle, carved in wood


### 2B — The Dining Room

A long dining table dominates this room, set for eight with tarnished silver cutlery and plates that still hold the remnants of a meal — petrified now, unrecognizable as food. Eight chairs. Only one has been disturbed — pushed back hard, one leg snapped, as though whoever sat in it left in tremendous haste or was pulled away. The china cabinet along the east wall is intact. One glass in it is cracked from floor to ceiling, as though exposed to sudden, intense pressure.

**GM Notes:** This was the last meal Aldric had as a human — he felt the change coming during dinner and fled to the cellar. The cracked glass resonates to low-frequency vibration — something large and loud broke it from inside the house. Players with a passive Perception of 13 or higher notice the dust pattern on the table shows handprints — normal-sized near the place settings, enormous and clawed at the head of the table.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of ancient food and tarnished metal
- Wind moans through a gap in the east wall plaster
- The candelabra on the table is silver — eight candles, eight burned to different heights

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Silver candelabra (worth 120 NC to a fence, 200 NC to a collector)


### 3 — The Servants' Passage

A narrow corridor behind the main rooms, running from the kitchen to the base of the servants' stair. The walls here are unplastered stone — utilitarian, meant for people not meant to be seen. Three hooks on the wall hold aprons. One apron has a large brown stain across the chest. The passage smells of mold and something sweeter underneath — herbs, dried and long past their use.

**GM Notes:** The stained apron belongs to the cook, who was the first person Aldric accidentally injured. The blood is old enough that no Witcher sense will identify the victim specifically, but the stain pattern suggests a defensive wound — the cook raised their arm. This is not a combat room but contributes to the horror texture of what happened here.

**Sensory Details:**
- Narrow enough that Indra's stone fists scrape the walls
- The sweet herb smell is dried wolfsbane — someone tried to use it as a ward


### 4A — The Kitchen

A large kitchen, surprisingly intact compared to the rest of the ground floor. The hearth is cold but the pots are still on their hooks. A bread oven sits in the north wall, its door hanging open. The work table in the center is covered with jars — preserves, dried herbs, rendered fat — most still sealed. Someone was stocking provisions here as though preparing for a long isolation. The back door to the yard is barred from inside with a beam twice the weight anyone normal could lift one-handed.

**GM Notes:** The provisions were Aldric's doing — after the first change, he locked himself in and tried to wait it out. The bar on the door required roughly 400lbs of lift to place — DC 20 Athletics to move it, and only someone with exceptional Strength can have put it there. The herb collection includes several legitimate alchemical components. DC 12 Arcana identifies wolfsbane, belladonna, and monk's hood — all folkloric 'cures' for lycanthropy, none of them effective.

**Sensory Details:**
- Still smells faintly of preserved food
- A large cast-iron pan has been bent — something struck the wall with it hard enough to deform the metal
- Mouse droppings on every surface

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Alchemical components (3x worth of basic reagents, value 30 NC total)
- Sealed preserves (food supply for 2 weeks for one person)


### 4B — The Larder

A cold larder off the kitchen. Stone shelves, a low ceiling. Something very large has been in here — one of the stone shelves is cracked through, a second has collapsed entirely. The floor is marked with claw gouges deep enough to catch a boot toe. In the corner, curled into itself: the skeleton of a dog, still collared, the collar leather stamped with a name. BREK.

**GM Notes:** Brek was Aldric's hunting dog. The dog did not flee — it stayed. Aldric killed it during an early transformation, probably accidentally, and has never re-entered this room since. Players who are paying attention will notice the claw marks are older than the ones elsewhere in the house — this happened first. DC 14 Medicine: the dog died from blunt trauma consistent with being struck by something very large. There's a tenderness to this room if players engage with it.

**Sensory Details:**
- Cold even by the manor's standards
- The collar leather is cracked but the name stamp is still clear
- No smell — too cold, too dry, too long ago


### 5 — The Entry Hall (Encounter Zone 2)

> [!warning] Encounter Zone
> ENCOUNTER ZONE 2 — Three Shades. Silver vulnerability. Low light.

The main hall opens here around the base of the grand staircase — a sweeping curve of dark wood, the banister carved with animals now barely recognizable under decades of grime. A large iron chandelier hangs overhead, three of its candles still holding wax stubs. The floor here is wrong in a way that takes a moment to identify: it's clean. Not cleaned recently, but clean as though nothing — dust, debris, insects — will settle here. The air is cold enough to see breath. Three shapes drift near the far wall, barely visible, like shadows cast by light that isn't there.

**GM Notes:** Three Shades (use Shadow stat block, MM p. 269). They are anchored to this hall and will not pursue beyond the doorways. They attack anyone carrying non-silver light sources first — their Strength Drain is the main threat. Silver weapons deal double damage. Lantern light or torchlight draws them; magical light or darkness makes them harder to spot. After combat, the chandelier candles will not relight — they haven't burned in decades. The 'clean floor' is because no organic material can settle in the shade-haunted zone.

**Sensory Details:**
- Visible breath even near the door
- The shadows move against the direction of any light source
- A low sound, not quite a voice, not quite wind

**Stat Block Reference:** Shadow — MM p. 269 × 3. Silver vulnerability added for Witcher tone.

## Second Floor

*The second floor is quieter than the ground floor in an unnatural way — the creaking of the stairs stops the moment you reach the landing. Sound behaves oddly here. Footsteps seem to come from one room ahead of wherever you actually are. The wallpaper on the landing — a repeating pattern of hunting scenes — has been defaced. Every hound in every scene has been scratched out.*


### 6 — The Landing Gallery

A wide landing with three doors leading off it and a fourth — narrower, with a different handle — that leads to the servants' stair. The wallpaper here carries the defacement from the staircase: every animal in the hunting pattern is scratched away. Only the humans remain, in their hunting poses, holding weapons over empty spaces where prey should be. A reading chair sits near the window with a book on the arm — a bestiary, open to the entry on bears.

**GM Notes:** The bestiary is Aldric's. The bear entry has been annotated in tight, cramped handwriting that gets progressively more erratic down the page. DC 12 Investigation reveals three distinct handwriting styles within the same notes — Aldric's mind was fracturing as he wrote. The annotations begin as academic observations and end as something more personal and anguished.

**Sensory Details:**
- Absolute quiet — no outdoor sounds reach this floor
- The chair's upholstery has been shredded on one arm, as though something gripped it through transformation

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Annotated bestiary (significant lore item — contains Aldric's early research into the amulet's effects)


### 7A — The Master Bedroom

A large bedroom with a four-poster bed, the hangings rotted to threads. The bed has never been slept in since the manor was abandoned — the coverlet is undisturbed except for dust. A writing desk in the corner holds correspondence that was never sent: three letters, sealed and addressed, stacked neatly as though awaiting collection. The wardrobe stands open and empty except for a single formal coat, a scholar's black, too fine for the countryside.

**GM Notes:** The three letters: one addressed to 'The Chapter' (the Brotherhood of Sorcerers, Aldric's former colleagues), one to 'Mira, at the Wehn farm outside Weddle' (his sister — referenced in the Study notes), one to Commander Varro's predecessor at the garrison by name. The letters are sealed. If opened: the Brotherhood letter is a confession and a request for help that was never answered. The Mira letter is a goodbye. The garrison letter is a warning about the amulet — written before Varro's time. Aldric was trying to do the right thing and ran out of time.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of old ink and something medicinal
- A pressed flower between the desk blotter and the surface — dried violet, crumbling

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Three sealed letters (significant lore items)
- Scholar's coat (quality garment, Cintran make)


### 7B — The Dressing Room

A small room off the master bedroom, meant for dressing and personal grooming. A mirror on the wall has been covered with a sheet. The vanity drawers have been emptied and the contents swept onto the floor: combs, buttons, a shaving kit. The floor here has one anomaly — a circular area about two feet across where the floorboards have been sanded smooth, all the old varnish stripped away. It sits in the center of the room and is very deliberately done.

**GM Notes:** The sanded circle is where Aldric worked a divination ritual trying to see his own future, hoping to find out if there was a cure. It failed — the magic he attempted here was beyond his ability in his deteriorating state. DC 15 Arcana to identify the ritual circle's purpose. The covered mirror: Aldric stopped being able to look at himself. The sheet has been there so long it's bonded to the mirror frame. If removed, the mirror reflects the room normally — except the player characters appear slightly wrong. Their shadows are bear-shaped.

**Sensory Details:**
- The mirror sheet moves in air currents despite no open window
- The sanded circle is the only clean surface in the room


### 8 — The Great Room (Encounter Zone 3)

> [!warning] Encounter Zone
> ENCOUNTER ZONE 3 — Ward puzzle. Touch open book then eye. Wrong sequence triggers spectral force damage.

A large room that once served as the manor's library anteroom and gathering space. Bookshelves line two walls, most of the books still present but warped and water-damaged. A reading table in the center holds a single open book — the only item in the room that appears pristine, as though protected. Above the fireplace mantle, a carved eye in the stonework stares toward the table. The room has a stillness to it that is qualitatively different from simple emptiness. The air resists movement, as though the space itself is holding its breath.

**GM Notes:** The ward: Aldric placed a protection ward on the passage to his Study. The sequence is touch the open book, then touch the carved eye. Wrong sequence: spectral force blast from the eye, DC 14 Dexterity save or 2d8 force damage, and the player is pushed 10 feet. Correct sequence: a soft click, and the door to the Study unlocks. The open book on the table is the key — it's open to a page about Conjunction-era ruins in Cintra. A clue Aldric left intentionally for anyone smart enough to follow his research. DC 13 Arcana identifies the ward's nature before triggering it. DC 10 Investigation: the dust on the floor shows footprints approaching the table, then the fireplace, in that order. Someone has done this before — Otrygg.

**Sensory Details:**
- The light is wrong — it seems to emanate faintly from the open book
- The carved eye has no pupils but follows movement
- Touching the ward incorrectly produces a smell of ozone and scorched stone


### 9 — The Study (Encounter Zone 4)

> [!warning] Encounter Zone
> ENCOUNTER ZONE 4 — Tripwire trap at door. Aldric's full notes. Letter to his sister Mira.

Aldric's study is the most intact room in the manor — whatever spirit of preservation protected the open book in the Great Room extends here. The desk is covered in notes, organized at first glance but increasingly chaotic on closer inspection. Diagrams of the amulet. Annotated magical theory. A timeline of symptoms. And underneath everything else, folded small and tucked under the ink jar: a letter addressed to Mira, unsealed. The bookshelves here are full of real texts — magical theory, Conjunction history, one volume that bears the Brotherhood's stamp on the spine. A tripwire runs across the doorway at shin height, connected to a jar of alchemical irritant on the lintel.

**GM Notes:** Tripwire: DC 13 Perception to spot. Triggered: the jar falls, DC 12 Dexterity save or blinded for 1 minute and disadvantage on all checks while clearing your eyes. Non-lethal, but miserable. Aldric's notes contain the full picture — what the amulet is, what it does, that he created the infection vector accidentally by wearing it too long while studying it. The letter to Mira: heartbreaking. He tells her he loves her, that the farm should be hers, that she should not come to the manor. He describes what he has become. He asks her to tell no one because he fears what the garrison will do. The Brotherhood volume is stamped as property of Ban Ard — stolen or borrowed, unclear. DC 14 Arcana: the notes describe a partial reversal procedure that requires the amulet to be destroyed — not delivered.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of old ink and burned candle wax — recently used by Otrygg
- The notes near the bottom of the pile are written in larger, shakier letters
- A single claw-mark on the desk edge — Aldric, during a bad moment

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Aldric's research notes (critical lore item — explains the amulet fully)
- Letter to Mira (critical lore item — the moral weight of the quest)
- Brotherhood volume from Ban Ard (worth 200 NC to a sorcerer, dangerous to possess)


### 10 — The Guest Room

A guest room that was occupied recently — within the last few months. Someone cleared the dust from the bed, made do with the moth-eaten blankets, and left a very deliberate arrangement of small objects on the windowsill: a river stone, a worn coin from Kaer Seren, a small carved figure of a bear. Otrygg's meditation objects. The room has the feeling of a place someone slept in knowing they might not sleep again.

**GM Notes:** Otrygg was here. The coin is Bear School currency — a specific denomination used in Kaer Seren for internal trade. The carved bear is a personal item. DC 10 Perception: the windowsill has four small scratched marks — Otrygg keeping track of days. He was here for four nights before he died. This room is purely atmosphere — it deepens the weight of Otrygg's death and the ghost encounter to come.

**Sensory Details:**
- The blankets are disturbed, pressed flat where someone slept
- The river stone is cold even by the manor's standards — it's been here a while
- A faint smell of witcher alchemicals — the same compounds as the kit in the yard

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Kaer Seren coin (small value, 5 NC, but significant to Spiros)

## Third Floor

*The third floor is where the house becomes something else. The stairs creak with a sound like something breathing. The temperature drops noticeably. Plaster has fallen from the ceiling in the corridor, and the exposed lath beneath looks like ribs. The doors up here are all closed. One of them, at the corridor's end, has been nailed shut from this side.*


### 11 — The Upper Landing

A narrower landing than the second floor, with a lower ceiling. The staircase continues up to the attic. The wallpaper here is different — older, a faded pattern of eyes. Not a decorative motif, not quite — more like someone drew eyes over and over on every surface they could reach, then eventually stopped. The eyes are all at different heights, some at floor level, some at ceiling level, as though drawn by a creature that changed size as it drew them.

**GM Notes:** The eyes were drawn by Aldric during his early transformations — he was documenting his changing perspective, literally. DC 12 Investigation: some eyes are drawn with fingernails rather than charcoal, and some are significantly larger than a human hand could produce. This is disturbing if players take a moment with it. The stairs to the attic are sound — no encounter here, just atmosphere.

**Sensory Details:**
- The sound of the stairs stops completely on this landing
- Visible scratch marks at varying heights on the doorframes


### 12A — The Locked Room

A room that was never a child's room despite what the furniture suggests — small bed, small desk, alphabet primer on the shelf. But the furniture is too old, unused for decades before the manor was abandoned. The primer on the shelf is not for a child learning letters. It's a mnemonic device — each letter paired with a symbol from a magical notation system. A tutor's tool for teaching hedge magic fundamentals. Aldric was self-taught. This was his first classroom.

**GM Notes:** This was the room of the manor's previous occupant, a hedge mage who tutored young Aldric in the basics. The magical primer is still usable — DC 12 Arcana and one week of study grants advantage on one future Arcana check of the player's choice. This is flavor primarily, but gives players something to take from the room. The small bed has a name carved into the headboard: PIOTR. Not Aldric. The previous teacher.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smaller than it should feel — everything slightly undersized
- The chalk markings on the small blackboard are still legible: magical notation

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Magical primer (study reward item — not monetizable, but useful)


### 12B — The Workroom

A workroom that bridges the two ends of the third floor corridor. Long worktable, tools of various kinds — carpentry, leatherwork, basic smithing. The tools are excellent quality but unused in years. On the table: a project begun and never finished — a repair to a piece of leather harness, the stitching half-done, needle still threaded. Someone stopped mid-task and never came back.

**GM Notes:** The half-finished harness is bear-sized. Aldric was modifying his own equipment during a lucid period, trying to make something he could wear in bear form. The project reveals he had moments of planning and hope between transformations. Passive Perception 14 notices the tools have been rearranged — some moved to accommodate hands much larger than the tools were designed for.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of leather oil and metal
- The needle is still threaded with heavy waxed cord

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Quality craftwork tools (value 80 NC as a set)


### 12C — The Storage Room

A storage room accessed from the workroom. Old furniture under canvas, trunks, a broken loom. One trunk has been forced open — the lock destroyed, the lid bent back. Inside: nothing. Whatever was here was taken or consumed. The canvas covers are marked with the same scratched-eye motif from the landing, but here the eyes are larger and angrier-looking.

**GM Notes:** The trunk held Aldric's emergency alchemical stockpile — potions for pain management, sedatives, things he was using to manage the transformation. All consumed. This was his last resource. The forced lock required enormous strength — the metal is bent, not picked. DC 10 to notice the size of the scratch marks on the trunk lid. Players can figure out the timeline: the stockpile ran out, and things got worse.

**Sensory Details:**
- Old canvas smell, dust, wood rot
- The eyes on the canvas seem to be watching the trunk


### 13 — The Nailed Room

At the end of the corridor, a door has been nailed shut from this side — six large nails driven in at angles, the work hasty but effective. Scratches on the door surface. The nails were driven from this side, meaning whoever nailed it shut was in the corridor — nailing something inside. Or, less comfortably, nailing themselves out. The wood around the frame on the other side of the door, visible through the gap, shows marks consistent with something very large pressing against it from within.

**GM Notes:** Inside (if players remove the nails — DC 14 Athletics or 10 minutes of work): a completely destroyed room. Bed frame bent in half. A window shutter torn away. The room is open to the outside. This is where Aldric first fully transformed — he was sleeping when it happened, woke up as something else, destroyed the room, escaped through the window, and then nailed the door shut from the corridor in a moment of subsequent lucidity. The nails are Aldric's attempt to create a boundary between what he was and what he had become.

**Sensory Details:**
- Low sound from behind the door — wind through the open window
- The nails are driven deep but unevenly — done in a hurry or in distress


### 14 — The Bathing Room

A bathing room with a copper tub, now heavily patinated, and a water cistern above it with a manual pump. The cistern still has water. The walls around the tub are covered in writing — small, cramped, frantic — at every height. Some of it is magical notation. Some of it is a personal journal. Some of it appears to be the same sentence written forty or fifty times: I AM STILL MYSELF.

**GM Notes:** The wall writing is a critical character moment for Aldric. Players who take time here learn: he was performing a ritual each morning to test his own cognition (the magical notation is a self-diagnostic), he was aware of his deterioration, and the mantra was both a hope and a lie. DC 12 Arcana reveals the diagnostic ritual. DC 14 Insight (reading the journal sections): Aldric mentions 'the woman in the village who knows about the old things' — this is a reference that could hook into a future quest about a hedge witch near Weddle.

**Sensory Details:**
- Water still in the cistern — cool, clean
- The writing becomes larger and more erratic higher on the wall, as though written when Aldric was taller than usual


### 15A — The Upper Sitting Room

A small sitting room at the stair end of the corridor, more intimate than the one below. Two chairs face a cold fireplace. A third chair has been added — larger, sturdier, its legs reinforced with iron bands. The fireplace mantle holds three items: a small painted portrait (a woman, smiling, with dark hair — Mira), a dried sprig of flowers, and a folded letter that has been opened and refolded many times.

**GM Notes:** The letter on the mantle is a reply from Mira — she wrote back. She doesn't know the full truth; Aldric told her he was ill, not what kind of ill. She writes that she is worried, that she wants to visit, that she loves him. It is dated roughly eight months ago. Aldric never replied to this letter. The oversized chair with iron bands is where he sat in his larger form during lucid periods. Passive Perception 12: the chair legs have left deep impressions in the floorboards. This room is the emotional core of the third floor — Aldric still loved his sister, still tried to maintain some domestic normalcy.

**Sensory Details:**
- Warmer than the rest of the floor — facing south, more sunlight hits the window
- The portrait is well-executed, clearly loved, slightly worn at the edges from handling

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Mira's letter (lore item — supports the moral weight of the quest)


### 15B — The Study Overflow

A small room clearly pressed into service as overflow for the study below — stacks of notes, empty ink bottles, discarded drafts that didn't make it to the study proper. Most of it is illegible or damaged. But on the floor, drawn in ink directly on the floorboards: a large diagram. A magical circle, incomplete. The tools to complete it — charcoal, chalk — lie discarded beside it.

**GM Notes:** The incomplete circle is Aldric's last serious attempt to construct a ritual that would destroy the amulet. He got most of the way through designing it before a transformation interrupted him and he was never lucid enough to return. DC 16 Arcana: the design is actually sound — with a complete version of these notes and one additional component (a source of silvered magical energy), the ritual would work. This seeds a possible resolution: destroy the amulet here rather than deliver it. But the component is missing and would need to be sourced.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of old ink — overwhelming in the small space
- The circle is drawn in a single unbroken line that runs off the edge of the room under the baseboard


### 15C — The Servant's Room

A servant's room, simply furnished — a cot, a chest, a hook for clothes. The chest has been opened and the contents removed carefully. On the cot: three coins, a small religious icon, and a note in a different hand from Aldric's. The note reads: GONE SOUTH. MIRA WILL KNOW. It is not signed.

**GM Notes:** The servant — the cook who survived — left this note for whoever came looking. They took their savings (coin count suggests 60 NC removed), their personal effects, and fled south toward the Slopes. The note's handwriting is shaky — the person was terrified when they wrote it. Mira (the sister) is the common thread now appearing multiple times. She becomes a hook for players who want to understand what happened here fully.

**Sensory Details:**
- Clean — relatively. Someone lived here carefully.
- The religious icon is to Melitele, goddess of fate and destiny — appropriate

## The Attic

*The attic is not where the danger lives, but it is where the house keeps its memories. Low beams, exposed rafters, light entering through gaps in the roof tiles in slanted columns that make the dust motes look like ash. The floor is more uncertain up here — test each step. The house shows its age most honestly in the attic.*


### 16 — The Attic Landing

The attic stairs open onto a central space with doors leading to the storage areas on either side. The ceiling here is the underside of the roof — exposed tiles, visible sky through two gaps where tiles have slipped. The floor is mostly sound but there are two sections near the east wall that flex underfoot with ominous intent. Dust has settled in layers here undisturbed for years. The first footprints in decades belong to the party.

**GM Notes:** The two weak floor sections: DC 10 Perception to notice before stepping on them. If stepped on: DC 12 Dexterity save or fall through to the third floor ceiling below — 2d6 bludgeoning damage and landing prone in whatever room is below. These are not trapped by design; the house is simply old. The undisturbed dust means no one has been up here since before Aldric's transformation — the attic is a true neutral zone in the manor.

**Sensory Details:**
- Wind through the roof gaps — the only fresh air in the manor
- Pigeons have nested in one corner, long since abandoned


### 17 — The Chest Room

South attic storage. Trunks, crates, wrapped furniture. A family's accumulated material history — before Aldric's time, from the look of it. An older coat of arms on one of the trunks: a silver stag on a blue field. Not Aldric's family. The manor was purchased, not inherited. Whoever lived here before left their possessions behind when they sold.

**GM Notes:** The previous family — the Vahn family, a minor noble house from Temeria — sold the manor to Aldric twenty years ago when they fell into debt. The coat of arms is recognizable with DC 12 History — the Vahn family is minor enough not to be widely known, but Spiros or Indra might encounter the name again in future quests. The trunks contain old clothes, household items, nothing of significant value. One trunk has a false bottom: DC 15 Investigation to find it, containing 200 NC in old Temerian coin (spendable but at 10% discount in Cintra).

**Sensory Details:**
- Old cloth and wood smell, dry
- Everything is covered in a uniform grey dust — time rather than neglect

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- False-bottom trunk: 200 NC in old Temerian coin (180 NC effective in Cintra)


### 18 — The Study Overflow (Attic)

A larger attic room that Aldric used for experiments too large or too dangerous for the study. A worktable, heavily scorched. Several large chalk circles on the floor — experimental, most of them incomplete or deliberately broken. The circles have been arranged in a sequence, showing progressive iterations of the same ritual design. The final version is the most complete. It matches the incomplete diagram on the third floor study overflow.

**GM Notes:** This confirms the ritual design in room 15B is Aldric's best work — the attic room shows twenty iterations leading to it. DC 14 Arcana: taken together, the attic iterations and the third floor design provide enough information that a player with Arcana proficiency could attempt to complete the amulet-destruction ritual with one additional component. This is the 'destroy it here' resolution path, now fully seeded. The worktable scorch marks are from experiments that went wrong, not from fire — they radiate outward from the center in a way that suggests magical discharge.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of old chalk dust and something burnt and metallic
- One chalk circle has never been erased — the lines are bright compared to the others


### 19 — The Lumber Room

Lumber and building materials — someone was mid-renovation when the manor was abandoned. New timber, still pale, stacked against the north wall. Iron nails in a barrel. A carpenter's tools, good quality. The lumber is dry and usable. Next to the timber stack, a coil of rope — thick, new, clearly bought at the same time as the renovation materials.

**GM Notes:** The renovation was Aldric's project — he was building something in the cellar (the containment space) and bought materials in advance. Players who examine the lumber and then see the cellar structure can connect the two. The rope has 50 feet and is in excellent condition. Practical item — the party will likely want it.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of fresh-cut wood, still present after years — the attic is very dry
- The carpenter's tools are laid out with the care of someone who respected their equipment

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- 50 feet of good rope
- Carpenter's tools (value 40 NC)


### 20 — The Servants' Attic

Servants' quarters converted to storage when the full staff left. Three cots folded against the wall. Three small chests, all open, all empty except one — which holds, carefully wrapped in cloth, a child's toy. A small carved horse, painted in bright colors that have faded but held. No child lived in this manor. The toy was someone's memory.

**GM Notes:** One of the servants brought this from their previous posting — a sentimental item, left behind in the chaos of departure. It humanizes the people who worked in this house. No mechanical value, no loot. Pure texture. If players are engaged with the human story of Old Hall, this moment resonates.

**Sensory Details:**
- Quieter than anywhere else in the manor
- The cots still have the imprints of bodies in the folded canvas


### 21 — The Crow's Nest

A small room at the manor's highest point, accessible through a hatch in the attic ceiling. A ladder leads up to a platform beneath the roof peak, with a shuttered window on each cardinal face. From here, on a clear day, you can see the Yaruga in the south, Brokilon's treeline to the east, and the smoke of Weddle village to the west. Nilfgaard's garrison camp is visible to the south — tents, a standard, smoke from cookfires. Commander Varro, waiting.

**GM Notes:** The crow's nest is a moment of breath in the descent — players can orient themselves geographically and emotionally before heading into the cellar. Seeing the garrison from up here is a deliberate reminder that Varro is real and waiting. DC 10 Perception: the garrison camp has grown since the party passed through — there's a second standard now, suggesting a senior officer arrived. This is not urgent, but it creates pressure. Depending on time of day: the sun is at a particular angle that throws long shadows from Brokilon. The forest looks close.

**Sensory Details:**
- The only place in the manor with natural light and fresh air
- Wind from the south smells of the Yaruga — river and distant plains
- Nilfgaard's black sun standard is visible if players look south

## The Undercroft

*The stairs to the cellar are stone, and they do not creak. The change in air is immediate — cold, damp, smelling of earth and something older than the house above. The cellar was not part of the original manor. It was excavated. The walls are rough-cut stone, not dressed, and they go down further than seems right for a country house. Something about the proportions is wrong — the cellar is too large, its ceilings too high. Aldric built himself a cage. He made it big enough to hold what he became.*


### 35 — The Cellar Stairs (Encounter Zone 5)

> [!warning] Encounter Zone
> ENCOUNTER ZONE 5 — Otrygg's remains. Ghost testimony trigger. Must find and acknowledge remains before ghost will speak.

The stairs descend to a landing before the main cellar opens. Halfway down, in a niche cut into the wall — possibly natural, possibly intentional — lie the remains of Otrygg of Kaer Seren. A witcher's leathers, silver-thread stitching at the collar (Bear School). The bones are scattered but present. His main gauche is still in his hand, grip locked by rigor and time. And standing at the base of the stairs, barely visible, looking up: a figure in the same leathers, translucent, not quite there.

**GM Notes:** Otrygg's ghost. He will not speak until his remains are acknowledged — the players must do something: move them to a dignified position, say a witcher's phrase over them, cover them with a cloak. Spiros will know the proper rite (automatic for a witcher). Once acknowledged: Otrygg can testify for up to 10 minutes of in-game time before he begins to fade. He knows: the amulet's infection mechanism, that Aldric wants to die and has asked repeatedly, that Varro sent him because the Empire wants the amulet as a weapon, and that there is a way to destroy it if someone completes the ritual in the attic. He does NOT know if Varro knows the full extent of what the amulet does. Otrygg is not hostile. He is tired. He failed this job and he knows it.

**Sensory Details:**
- The temperature drops significantly on the stairs
- Otrygg's ghost does not cast a shadow
- The main gauche in his hand is a fine weapon — silver alloy blade

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Otrygg's main gauche (fine silver alloy weapon, +1 to hit, silvered — Bear School work)
- Otrygg's witcher medallion (personal item, not the quest amulet)


### 26 — The Antechamber

A rough-cut antechamber at the base of the stairs. The walls here are marked with the same eye-symbol from the upper floors, but here they are larger, drawn at height and at floor level both. Iron rings are set into the walls — large ones, the kind used to tether draft animals. The chains are gone. The rings have been pulled at hard enough to deform the stone around them. And in the center of the floor: a feeding bowl, enormous, and beside it: a book, still open, its pages swollen with moisture but legible. It is a collection of philosophical dialogues. Someone left it open here for a creature that might, on some days, be able to read.

**GM Notes:** This room shows the tragedy of Aldric's situation — he set up a space where his other self could live, left books and food for it, tried to maintain his own humanity by providing for what he was becoming. The iron rings were where he tried to chain himself during transformations — they didn't hold. The book is open to a dialogue about the nature of the self. This is Aldric's question to himself: am I still myself? The room has no encounter, only weight.

**Sensory Details:**
- Smell of damp earth and animal musk — recent
- The feeding bowl has been used recently
- Breathing can be heard from deeper in the cellar

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Philosophical dialogues (lore item — marked and annotated by Aldric)


### 27 — The Warren

Side passages branch off the main cellar corridor — some natural fissures in the rock, some excavated. All lead back to the main space eventually. The walls here are scratched from floor to ceiling with claw marks. Not in anger — in pattern. Geometric. Repeated. As though something was working through a problem by marking the walls, pacing and thinking in a language of scratches.

**GM Notes:** The claw-mark patterns in the warren are Aldric attempting mathematics and magical notation in his bear form. He was working on the ritual even then, in the body that couldn't hold a pen. DC 15 Arcana: the patterns are a valid (if crude) attempt to solve the missing component problem for the ritual. They represent approximately six months of work. This is devastating once players understand what they're looking at.

**Sensory Details:**
- The scratches are at two heights — human reach and much higher
- Some passages are too narrow for anything large to have passed through — but the claw marks go everywhere


### 28 — The Sealed Passage

A heavy door set into the east wall, barred with an iron beam and two padlocks. The door has been reinforced from this side with additional timber. It leads to a natural cave passage that, based on the slope of the floor and the sound of water, eventually connects to the Yaruga tributary system. Someone built this as an emergency exit — or an emergency entry seal.

**GM Notes:** Aldric built the sealed passage as an escape route in case he lost control entirely and needed to flee the manor without going through the house. He barred it from inside — the seals are his. The passage leads to a river cave about half a mile east. Players who open it (DC 15 Athletics to remove the beam, then pick or break two padlocks) gain a back route in and out of the manor — and Aldric, in the boss fight, might use it to attempt escape if things go badly for him.

**Sensory Details:**
- Sound of water through the door — distant, rhythmic
- Cold air seeping through the gap — the passage connects to the outside


### 29 — The Trap Field

The approach to the main cellar chamber is crossed by a series of pressure-stone traps — flat stones set into the floor that trigger when weight bears on them. The stones are not quite flush with the surrounding floor. They look almost natural. Aldric set these to slow intruders — or possibly to slow himself, if he came this way in the wrong body.

**GM Notes:** Five pressure stones, each marked X on the map. DC 13 Perception to spot (can be done once per 10-foot section). Triggered: iron bars drop from the ceiling, blocking passage and ringing loudly enough to alert Aldric. The bars can be lifted (DC 18 Athletics) or one can be broken (AC 15, 20 HP). The noise is the real danger — Aldric becomes aware of intruders and is not surprised in the boss fight. If the party gets through without triggering any traps, they get a full surprise round on Aldric.

**Sensory Details:**
- The floor stones have a slightly different color from the surrounding rock
- A faint smell of rust — the bars above have been waiting a long time


### 31 — The Shrine

A natural alcove off the main corridor, made into something deliberate. A flat stone serves as an altar. On it: a small figurine of Melitele, goddess of destiny and healing. Two candles — burned entirely to nothing. And between the candles: the amulet. The Bear School medallion, sitting openly on the altar stone, its secondary enchantment visible to witcher senses as a deep amber glow. Someone placed it here as an offering or a marker. Around the alcove walls: written in charcoal, in a careful hand, a single sentence repeated until the wall ran out of space: THIS IS WHAT I MADE. THIS IS WHAT I MUST UNDO.

**GM Notes:** The amulet's location. It is not locked or guarded here — Aldric placed it at the shrine as a kind of penance. He comes here in lucid moments to look at it and remember what he did. Players can take it freely. If they do, Aldric will sense its removal within minutes (he feels the separation from the object) and the boss fight begins on his terms — he finds them rather than them finding him. This is mechanically significant: taking the amulet without confronting Aldric first removes the party's positional advantage. Spiros can see the secondary enchantment (amber glow) with his witcher senses even without the medallion trigger.

**Sensory Details:**
- The altar stone is clean — maintained
- Candle wax has pooled and hardened many times over
- The charcoal writing is in multiple layers — written, faded, written again

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- THE AMULET (quest objective — Bear School medallion with infection enchantment)


### 33 — The Cellar Proper (Boss Chamber)

> [!warning] Encounter Zone
> ENCOUNTER ZONE 6 — Aldric (CR 4 Werebear). He wants to die. Moral weight: he may ask for it directly.

The main cellar is vast — Aldric excavated far beyond any reasonable need, as though the house-builder impulse of his lucid self was trying to give the creature-self enough room to live. Rough stone walls, a ceiling fifteen feet high. Evidence of habitation everywhere — a depression in the earth where something large has slept repeatedly, the bones of large animals (deer, boar, a horse), a half-eaten side of something that was once living. And in the center of the room, sitting with his back against the far wall, human-shaped for the moment: Aldric. Middle-aged. Soft hands. A scholar's face — the same face from the portrait in the receiving hall. He looks up when the party enters. He does not run.

**GM Notes:** Aldric begins the encounter in human form. His opening line, regardless of how the party enters: 'You found the medallion, then. Otrygg didn't manage it.' He knows Otrygg is dead. He is not surprised by non-humans — he's been alone long enough not to care about that. His priorities in conversation: he wants to die, he wants the ritual used to destroy the amulet, and he does not want his sister told how he died (only that he died peacefully, which is a lie he wants told). If the party agrees to destroy the amulet, he will cooperate with the ritual. If the party intends to deliver the amulet to Varro, he transforms and fights — not to escape, but to destroy the amulet himself. He cannot be talked into willingly giving it to the Empire. Combat: CR 4 werebear, modified. See stat block. He will not deal killing blows — he fights to disable or to be killed. If reduced to 0 HP in humanoid form, he asks the party to finish it. Moral resolution is entirely in the players' hands.

**Sensory Details:**
- Aldric smells of blood and earth and old books
- His eyes are slightly too gold — bear-amber at the edges
- The sleeping depression in the earth is enormous — twenty feet across

**Loot / Notable Items:**
- Post-encounter: Aldric's personal notes (already found in study)
- His formal signet ring — the Wehn family seal (sentimental value, 20 NC melt value)
- His key ring — including the key to the sealed passage (room 28)


### 36 — The Water Passage

A section of the cellar floor where the water table has intruded — two inches of cold, dark water covering about thirty feet of passage. The water is perfectly still. The passage is narrow. Something has moved through here recently — the silt on the bottom is disturbed.

**GM Notes:** No encounter, but Indra's stone fists and forearms make crossing awkward — she's heavier on that side and the uneven footing requires a DC 10 Athletics or Acrobatics check or she slips and goes down into the water. Not dangerous, mildly embarrassing, potentially a good character moment. The disturbed silt is from Aldric in bear form using this passage as a water source.

**Sensory Details:**
- Cold water, completely silent
- The passage narrows enough that large characters must turn sideways


### 37 — The Collapse

A section of the cellar that has partially collapsed — rock fall from the ceiling, a section of wall buckled inward. The collapse is old, predating Aldric's occupation. Beyond the rubble, barely accessible, is a small natural cave. Old — much older than the manor. The walls of the cave show marks that are not claw-marks and not writing. They are symbols. Pre-Conjunction. Whatever was here, was here before humans came to this part of the Continent.

**GM Notes:** A seed for future content only. The pre-Conjunction markings are real — this cave was a site of some significance to whoever (whatever) lived here before. DC 15 History or DC 15 Arcana to recognize the markings as pre-Conjunction. No encounter, no loot, no immediate consequence. If the GM wants to develop this later, the cave connects to something older and stranger. For now it simply establishes that Old Hall sits on ground with history.

**Sensory Details:**
- The cave is dry and cold beyond the collapse
- The symbols do not look like anything in any known tradition


### 38 — The Reflection Pool

The cellar's lowest point holds a natural pool of still water, fed by seepage through the rock. The pool is perfectly circular — too perfectly for nature, as though the stone around it was shaped by something. The water is clear to the bottom: eight feet deep, nothing in it, the bottom bare stone. At the center of the pool, rising just above the water line: a flat stone platform, dry despite the surrounding water. The platform is scored with circular grooves — a natural rock formation, or perhaps not. If the party stands at the pool's edge, they see their reflections — but the reflections are wrong. Distorted. Larger.

**GM Notes:** The reflection pool is where Aldric would have performed the amulet-destruction ritual, had he been able to complete it. The platform is the natural terminus of all the ritual work in the house above. The distorted reflections (showing the party as larger, rougher, more animal — bear-adjacent) are a side effect of the amulet's ambient magic suffusing this lowest space. If the party completes the ritual here, using the attic notes and the required silvered magical component: the amulet can be safely destroyed in the pool. This is the intended resolution path for players who want to act against Varro's commission. After destruction, the reflections normalize.

**Sensory Details:**
- Perfect silence — even sound behaves differently at the pool
- The water tastes of stone and iron
- Reflections appear on a slight delay — half a second behind actual movement

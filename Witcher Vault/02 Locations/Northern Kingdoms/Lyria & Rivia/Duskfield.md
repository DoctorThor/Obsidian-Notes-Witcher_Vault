# Description
Duskfield is a remote, fog-drenched woodland village perched at the edge of the **Duskhollow Forest**, one of the darkest stretches of woodland in the borderlands.  
It is small, isolated, and perpetually wary. Every villager knows someone who has vanished in the mist, and every family keeps charms or talismans to ward off the forest’s spirits.

The village is held together by shared fear, mutual labor, and stubborn pride.  
It is a place where **every face is familiar**, and every absence is felt.

---

# Geography
Duskfield stands on:
- a low rise above boggy moorland  
- the northern fringe of Duskhollow Forest  
- a narrow dirt road connecting it to Brugge (2–3 days)  

**Environment:**  
Dense fog, cold mornings, wet summers, early frosts.

**Nearby hazards:**  
- Wolves  
- Water hags in the marsh  
- Forest spirits deeper in the woods  
- Leshen territory many miles east (but villagers fear it’s closer)

---

# Districts
Being a **small village**, Duskfield has functional clusters rather than districts:

**Village Center** — meeting fire, Alderman’s hut, communal well.  
**Timber Yards** — sawpits, stacked lumber, lumberjacks' cabins.  
**Hunter’s Row** — smokehouse, bowsmith, traps, wolf pelts.  
**Riverside Huts** — peat-diggers, reed collectors, small ferry.  
**Oldhomes** — crooked, aging houses of the original founding families.

---

# Government
Duskfield is governed by:
- **Alderman Jorvik Helden**, chosen by tradition  
- **Four village elders**, representing main families  

Law is informal and based on custom.  
Punishments aim to protect the community — **fines, labor, or exile to the woods** for severe crimes.

No official oversight from Aedirn except rare tax collectors.

---

# Defenses
The village is lightly defended but vigilant.

- A **waist-high timber wall** around the center of the village  
- Two rickety watchtowers overlooking the forest line  
- **The Lantern Watch**, a rotating night patrol of 8–10 villagers  
- A handful of hunters with bows  
- Every home owns at least one spear or hunting axe  

Major threats (drowners, wolves, single monsters) often require help from witchers.

---

# Economy
Duskfield survives on **simple frontier labor**:

**Primary industries:**  
- Logging (low-scale)  
- Hunting and trapping  
- Herb gathering (plants used in potions and alchemy)  
- Peat harvesting  
- Minimal fishing and river ferrying  

**Trade:**  
Two or three caravans visit per month, mostly from Brugge.

**Economic dangers:**  
- Food scarcity in bad winters  
- Lost workers due to monsters  
- Risky reliance on forest resources

# History

**Founding:**  
Established as a logging camp; became a village when settlers built homes and local shrines.

**Tracked Events:**  
- “The Fog Year” — several villagers vanished over one summer.  
- The Burning of the First Mill — cause never discovered.  
- The Hunter’s Stand Massacre — wolves or something bigger attacked.  
- Survived two Nilfgaard scouting incursions.  

Many records lost to fire or time.

---

# Culture
Duskfielders are:
- suspicious of outsiders  
- respectful of the forest  
- superstitious but not fanatical  
- fiercely communal  

Daily life revolves around small traditions:
- Fog Lantern Vigil at dusk  
- Offerings of bread or berries left at the forest edge  
- Elders telling stories of spirits  
- Shared hunts for food and safety  

Villagers behave pragmatically — survival first, niceties second.

---

# Religion
**Melitele** is the official and dominant faith.

Unofficially, villagers also appease:
- forest dwelling spirits  
- old totems said to predate the Conjunction  
- ancestral guardians  

Shrines are humble: carved wood, stones in rings, antler bundles.

The Eternal Fire **is distrusted** and has no presence here.

# Notable Locations
- **The Old Bark Inn** — hearth, simple meals, gossip hub.  
- **Alderman’s Hut** — meeting hall, small archive of local lore.

- **Fogwalker’s Ferry** — single ferry crossing the Duskhollow River.  
- **The Stag Trail** — leads into the deep forest; avoided after sunset.

# Notable NPCs
- **Jorvik Helden** — Alderman, cautious and weary.  
- **Sister Brenna** — healer and priestess, highly respected.  
- **Branik Stonesinew** — chief hunter, local hero, hides a dark past.  
- **Sada Vellin** — innkeeper, widowed, source of rumors.  
- **Vellios** — an elf herbalist living on the outskirts, mistrusted by many.  
- **Ervec the Ferryman** — quiet, pale, rumored to see the dead.

# Organizations & Factions
**Logging Crew** — eight lumberjacks risking the forest daily.  
**Lantern Watch** — rotating night patrol to spot threats early.  
**Hunters’ Lodge** — informal group; protectors and trackers.  
**Swamp Runners** — small, illicit group moving goods via marsh.  
**Forest Supplicants** — very small group secretly venerating local totems.

4. The timber mill fire was no accident.  
5. The Lantern Watch heard singing deep in the woods.

# **1. The Core Concept (1 Sentence Rule)**

Every weapon and piece of armor has a **Durability Score (DS)** from **0 to 5**, and events in the world reduce it.  
Lower DS = penalties; 0 DS = broken or useless.

That’s it.

---

# **2. Durability Score (DS) Track**

|DS|Condition|Effect|
|---|---|---|
|**5**|Pristine|No penalties|
|**4**|Worn|No effect (cosmetic only)|
|**3**|Chipped / Scuffed|–1 to attack rolls OR –1 AC (armor)|
|**2**|Damaged|–1 to damage OR –2 AC|
|**1**|Badly Damaged|Weapon: roll with disadvantage; Armor: AC reduced by 3|
|**0**|Broken|Weapon unusable; Armor provides no AC|

This mirrors Witcher 3’s “green-yellow-red” but simplifies it for TTRPG use.

---

# **3. What Causes Durability Loss?**

_(These are triggers you can drop in during play.)_

### **Weapons lose 1 DS when:**

- You crit fail an attack
    
- You hit a creature with **stone skin, armor plates, thick carapace, etc.**
    
- You fight **3 encounters** without maintenance
    
- You damage the weapon intentionally (e.g., prying open something)
    

### **Armor loses 1 DS when:**

- An attack deals **critical damage** to the wearer
    
- A hit exceeds **half the character’s max HP**
    
- The wearer suffers fire/acid/corrosive effects
    
- They take **3 encounters** worth of hits without rest
    

### **Tools lose 1 DS when:**

- Used in harsh terrain
    
- Used repeatedly in downtime
    
- Misused (lockpicks on heavy locks, rope friction burns, etc.)
    

---

# **4. Repair Rules (Simple & Witcher-Themed)**

### **Quick Repairs (field maintenance)**

- Restore **1 DS**
    
- Takes **10 minutes**
    
- Costs **5–10₡** in oils, whetstones, sewing kits, etc.
    
- Can’t raise above DS 4
    

### **Full Repairs (blacksmith/armorer)**

- Restore to **DS 5**
    
- Takes **1–2 hours**
    
- Costs:
    
    - **Weapons:** 20–60₡
        
    - **Armor:** 30–80₡
        
- Mastercrafted gear: +50% cost
    

### **Magical Repairs**

For enchanted / relic items:

- Takes **30 minutes**
    
- Costs **10₡ × item rarity tier**
    
- Requires someone with arcane knowledge
    

_(Magic solves a problem but at cost.)_

# **5. Optional: Quality Determines Maximum DS**

You can add realism: better items withstand more.

|Item Quality|Max DS|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**Crude / Rusted**|3|Bandit junk|
|**Common**|4|Town-smith goods|
|**Quality Steel / Reinforced Armor**|5|Standard|
|**Mastercrafted**|6|Witcher gear, dwarven marvels|
|**Relic / Enchanted**|7|Ancient or magical items|

If DS drops below 0 → item is **destroyed**.

---

# **6. Optional: Quick Maintenance Between Encounters**

After every _encounter_, players may spend **1 minute**:

- Sharpening blades
    
- Oiling joints
    
- Straightening dents
    

If they do, they **ignore the 3-encounter wear rule**.

If they don’t, items degrade.

This rewards Witcher-like preparation.

# **7. The GM Quick Table (Use at Table)**

**When to apply wear?**  
If any of these happens → **-1 DS**:

- Crit fail
    
- Take/Deal crit
    
- Fight armored/monstrous foes
    
- 3 encounters without care
    
- Fire/acid/corrosive
    
- Harsh misuse
    

**Penalty?**  
Look at the DS value.

**Repair?**  
5–10₡ in field, 20–80₡ in town.

Done.

# **8. Example in Play**

Geralt’s longsword (DS 5) fights 3 ghouls → no special events.  
Next fight is against an armored necrophage with stone plates → –1 DS.

Sword now **DS 4** ("worn").

Later:

- Crit fail vs a noonwraith → –1 DS  
    Now **DS 3** → –1 to attack rolls.
    

Geralt pays an armorer 40₡, 1 hour → back to **DS 5**.

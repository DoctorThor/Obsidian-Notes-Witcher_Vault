## 1. Core Formula (GM Cheat Line)

For any item:

> **Final Buy Price** = Base Price × Region Mod × Quality Mod × Rarity Mod  
> **Final Sell Price** = Final Buy Price × Vendor Rate × Haggling Mod

Where each “Mod” is a multiplier like **1.20** for +20%, **0.80** for –20%, etc.

You can ignore any mods you don’t care about in the moment.

## 3. Quality Mod (Condition / Craftsmanship)

Choose **one**:

|Quality / Condition|Modifier|
|---|---|
|Ruined / barely usable|×0.50|
|Worn / chipped / dented|×0.75|
|Standard / serviceable|×1.00|
|Fine craftsmanship|×1.25|
|Mastercrafted / witcher-grade|×1.50|

You can use this for **weapons, armor, tools, or even clothing**.

---

## 4. Rarity Mod (How hard is it to find?)

Choose **one** if relevant (or just skip for common stuff):

|Rarity|Modifier|
|---|---|
|Common|×1.00|
|Uncommon / restricted|×1.25|
|Rare (needs contacts)|×1.50|
|Very rare / contraband|×2.00|

Use this mainly for:

- magic-ish items
    
- specialized gear
    
- forbidden books
    
- rare alchemy components

## 5. Vendor Rates & Haggling

### Vendor Base Buy Rate (What they pay the PCs)

Pick a default depending on how harsh you want your world:

|Style|Vendor Rate|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**Harsh Witcher**|×0.30|They fleece you.|
|**Moderate**|×0.40|Good balance.|
|**Generous**|×0.50|More heroic than Witcher.|

I’d suggest: **0.40** as default.

### Haggling Mod (Negotiation)

Apply this **after** vendor rate, based on a social check:

|Result / Roleplay|Haggling Mod|
|---|---|
|Failed / bad impression|×0.90|
|Normal transaction|×1.00|
|Solid success (good roll/roleplay)|×1.10|
|Huge win (critical / great leverage)|×1.15|

For **buying** (PCs pay): flip the sign mentally:

- Great deal: multiply price by 0.90–0.85
    
- Normal: ×1.00
    
- Bad deal: ×1.10
    

---

## 6. Quick Step-By-Step Use at Table

**When NPC sells to PCs (buy price):**

1. Start with **Base Price** (from your Witcher-priced table).
    
2. Apply **Region Mod** (based on where they are).
    
3. Adjust for **Quality** (ruined / fine / mastercrafted).
    
4. Optionally adjust for **Rarity** if special.
    
5. Optionally tweak for **haggling** (discount or markup).
    
6. **Round to nearest 1₡ or 5₡** for sanity.
    

**When NPC buys from PCs (sell price):**

1. Calculate **Buy Price** as above (what it’s worth retail).
    
2. Apply **Vendor Rate** (e.g., ×0.40).
    
3. Apply **Haggling Mod** if they negotiate.
    
4. Round to nearest 1₡ or 5₡.

## 7. Worked Examples

### Example 1: Buying a Longsword in Velen

- Base longsword price: **250₡**
    
- Region: **Velen** → weapons/armor **×1.20**
    
- Quality: standard **×1.00**
    
- Rarity: common **×1.00**
    

**Calculation:**

- 250₡ × 1.20 = **300₡** (final price)
    

If PCs bargain well (10% discount):

- 300₡ × 0.90 = **270₡**
    

---

### Example 2: Selling a Mastercrafted Sword in Novigrad

- Base sword: 250₡
    
- Quality: mastercrafted **×1.50**
    
- Region: Novigrad **×1.00**
    
- Rarity: rare **×1.50**
    
- Vendor rate: **0.40**
    
- Haggling: good success **×1.10**
    

**Step 1 – Retail value:**

- 250₡ × 1.50 (quality) = 375₡
    
- 375₡ × 1.50 (rarity) = **562.5₡** → call it **560₡**
    

**Step 2 – Vendor offer:**

- 560₡ × 0.40 = 224₡
    
- 224₡ × 1.10 ≈ **246₡**
    

So the PCs might be offered **around 245–250₡**.

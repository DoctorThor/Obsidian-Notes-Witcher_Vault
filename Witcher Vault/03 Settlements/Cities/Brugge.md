# 🏙️ Overview
Brugge is a humble frontier town between Lower Aedirn and northern Velen, built along a bend in the river. Though modest, its sawmills and timber trade make it a vital point of commerce.  
It is overshadowed by the dark, ancient forests that surround it — places where villagers do not wander without charms, stories whisper of spirits, and where John of Brugge once built his mysterious tower.  
Brugge is tense, wary, and proud of surviving on a land that seems eager to swallow it.

---

# 🗺️ Geography & Layout
- **Terrain:** Surrounded by dense old-growth forest; river cutting east–west  
- **Climate:** Cool, damp, foggy mornings; harsh winters  
- **Roads:** One main road from Velen, one trade road toward Aedirn  
- **Forest:** The Duskhollow — rumored haunted, dangerous, and very old  

**Districts:**
- **River Quarter:** Docks, ferry, fishermen, riverside markets  
- **Market Square:** Butchers, merchants, craftsmen, notice board  
- **South Wall:** Guard barracks, stables, sawmills  
- **Old Brugge:** Crooked homes, hedges, shrines; the original settlement  
- **Forest Edge:** Hunters’ cabins, tanneries, logging camps  

---

# 🏰 Government & Politics
Brugge is ruled nominally by an **Elder Council** — a collection of older townsfolk from the founding families — but practical power rests with **Castellan Hrodvir**, who commands the guard and controls river tolls.

**Political tensions:**
- Logging guild wants to expand farther into the Duskhollow  
- Old families argue this will anger the forest spirits  
- Eternal Fire preachers have recently arrived, stoking conflict  
- Rumors of Scoia’tael activity make the guard paranoid  

Corruption is mild but growing — mostly bribes, toll-dodging, and smuggling.

---

# 💰 Economy & Trade
Brugge thrives on:
- **Timber** (major industry)  
- **Game & furs**  
- **Herbalists** selling forest reagents  
- **River trade** to Aedirn & Velen  

Notable issues:
- Recent disappearances decreased hunter output  
- Trade ships avoid Brugge in fog season  
- Sawmills operate on thin margins due to monsters in the woods  

---

# 📜 History
- Founded ~300 years ago as a hunting outpost  
- Expanded into a timber town after Aedirn wars  
- The **Tower of John of Brugge** was built 150 years ago by a reclusive mage  
- After John’s disappearance, the tower sealed itself and became feared  
- Several “forest tragedies” over the decades (lost children, hunters vanishing)  
- A drought 40 years ago led to partial famine  
- Recent years saw an uptick in monster sightings
